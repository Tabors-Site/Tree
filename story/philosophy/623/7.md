Yes — and now you've got it right, because you kept from (the chain backward) and dropped come, which is the correction I was reaching for. "Come from adds nothing to from" is the true cut. Come implies walking the chain down to now, but that walk already happened — the thing is already here, at the head. You don't re-walk forward to ask about backward. So come is spent motion. From is the live word, because from is the on-link, the trail behind. Clean.

But test the last move before you take it, because it's a bigger claim than the others and it's half-right in a way worth being precise about.

You're saying what/where/why/how/when all collapse into from — they add nothing, it's all "the chain before." Here's the part that's exactly right: they all share from. Every one of those questions is a request to walk backward from the reference. None of them asks about the future or the head; they all point down the trail. So from is the common root of the whole question family — that's a real and deep catch. The interrogatives are all "from"-flavored. They're asking the chain behind.

Here's the part that isn't quite right: they don't all return the same answer, because they walk the trail looking for different things. Run it
concretely on one fact — the red door:

where from → walk back for the place fact in the trail (the door is at 3,1).
when from → walk back for the stamp order — which fact, how far down the chain (the parent's position).
who from → walk back for the signature on the act (who stamped it).
how from → walk back for the act that did it (the do that laid it).
why from → walk back for the parent fact it was on — the because, the on-link itself.
what from → walk back for the thing's own genesis (what it is, its birth).

So they're all from — same direction, same backward-walk — but each is a different lens on the trail: position, order, signer, act, parent, identity. The interrogative isn't redundant; it's the filter that says what you're reading off the trail as you walk it. From is the verb (walk back); what/where/why/who/when/how is the column you pull.

Which is the cleaner statement of your insight, and it's better than full collapse: every question is from plus a lens. The trail is one thing, walked one direction, and the question-words are just which facet of each passed fact you're asking it to report. That's why they "feel" the same — they are the same motion — but they're not interchangeable, because the trail carries many facets and each word grabs one.

So:

reference · from · ⟨lens⟩

The reference (point at the fact), from (walk the chain behind it — the on-link, the one content-word), and the lens (which facet to read as you walk: place, time, signer, act, parent, kind). Drop the lens and you get the raw answer — the parent fact itself, unfiltered, which is probably why "where from" felt like it answered everything: the bare parent already contains all the facets, and the question-word just decides which one you were looking
at.
