Continuity successor to 16.md. 16.md made all four BE ops `.word`s with `kind:"beop"` (truename, death,

checked by a 16-agent workflow (5 readers, 3 design approaches judged by 3 lenses, 4 skeptics).

## The insight: the verb is a property of the word, recovered from the fold

Tabor's reframe (16.md): *"see ops are really the scribed NOUNS (data shapes that render), not verbs — the
verb-ness came from wrapping them in `can-see` (the permission)."* Run it to ground:

- **SEE is a noun-read.** All ten see-ops (`llm-connections`, `llm-chain`, `http-stats`, `connections`,
  `capture-template`, `capture-being`, `clones`, `verify-reel`, `verify-act`, `chain-root`) return a data
  shape — a descriptor, a catalog, a verification report — and stamp **no fact**. SEE perceives; it does
  not change the world.
- **DO / BE / NAME are word-speaking.** Each stamps exactly one fact via `emitFact`. `do.js:278` hardcodes
  `verb:"do"`, the BE emit sites (`be.js:449/505/565/616`) carry `verb:"be"`, `name.js:162` carries
  `verb:"name"`. But the handler comes from the fold by **name**, and the fact's `act` comes from the
  word's `factAction` (`do.js:279`, the BE `.word`'s `Return factParams`, `name.js:158`). The verb string
  is a label glued on per-file, not a routing decision.

And the kind is **already there**. Every binding in the one shared projection (`_projection`,
`wordStore.js:134`) carries its `kind`: `op` (do), `beop` (be), `nameop` (name), `seeop` (see), plus
`ableword` / `reducer` / `type` / `concept`. The four wire verbs each run a near-identical resolver —
`resolveDoOpFromFold` (178), `resolveBeOpFromFold` (458), `resolveNameOpFromFold` (410),
`resolveSeeOpFromFold` (498) — that looks up the prefixed name (`be:<op>` / `name:<op>` / `see:<op>` /
bare) and checks `w.kind`. The verb is just a pre-filter choosing which of four resolvers runs. **The word
it resolves to already carries the answer the verb was encoding.**

So: the verb is redundant with the word's kind. The fold carries the kind. The collapse is not "build a new
dispatcher" — it is "stop forking on a verb where the fork is already redundant, and admit the two places
where the fork is REAL."

## The reduction: see · fact · call (be + name dissolve into fact-on-a-type)

Three wire channels — but NOT three symmetric acts (see §"One act, the stamp"): one stamps, one reads, one
stamps-and-awaits.

1. **SEE / render** — read a noun, return a descriptor, stamp **no fact**. `kind:seeop`, plus a raw-position
   place read (`see.js` `buildPlaceDescriptor`). The inert one.
2. **FACT** — stamp **one** fact on a noun-TYPE. `kind` in `{op, beop, nameop}`. The noun-type the fact
   lands on is carried by the **word**, not the verb:
   - `op` → space / matter / being (`resolveAuditTarget`, `do.js:281`)
   - `beop` → being (`of:{kind:"being"}`, `be.js`; the `BEING_ONLY_TARGET_VERBS` guard, `facts.js:77`)
   - `nameop` → name (`of:{kind:"name"}`, `name.js:165`)
3. **CALL** — the stamp **+ an await**. Its write side is a being-targeted fact (one act); the round-trip
   pairs it with an awaited FOREIGN stamp (the receiver decides + stamps their own; you fold the reply). The
   await is WIRE, not a second act (see §"where it runs out").

**BE and NAME are not their own verbs.** They are FACT-on-a-noun-TYPE — `do` on the identity layer.
`be:connect` is "a fact-word whose noun is `being`"; `name:declare` is "a fact-word whose noun is `name`."
This matches the ground exactly: the BE `.word`s already return `factParams`/`factTarget` and the dispatcher
stamps (16.md). `name.js`'s `writeNameFact` (`name.js:148-170`) is structurally `do.js`'s `emitFact` with
`of:{kind:"name"}` instead of `resolveAuditTarget`. The closed BE set (birth / connect / release / switch /
death) + truename and the NAME set (declare / banish / connect / release / set-password) are all FACT-words
distinguished only by the noun-type their word declares.

**The noun-type IS the I-vs-story axis (Tabor): `be` is I-facts, `do` is story-facts.** The split inside FACT
is not arbitrary — it is the same left/right stance split (`project_ibpa_stance_attribution_doctrine`)
surfacing at the fact layer. BE/NAME stamp on the **I** (identity: being, name — the left-stance actor/self);
DO stamps on the **story** (the world: space, matter — the right-stance reality). So FACT reads as *I-fact
(be/name) | story-fact (do)*, split by whether the noun is the self or the world. That is why be+name dissolve
cleanly into fact: they are facts whose noun is the I, the same way do is a fact whose noun is the story.

**The verb namespace stays on the wire forever; the language collapses its dispatch to three.** The wire
verb set is `{see, do, call, be, type}` (`envelope.js:36`) — NAME is **not** a wire verb (it reaches
`nameVerb` via the stamper/momentum path and has a separate `socket.on('name')` session channel,
`nameSession.js`). `fact.verb` is in the fact hash (`hash.js:81`), so history is verb-stamped and immutable;
the wire cannot relabel past facts. The verb survives as a **transport hint + address-contract selector**,
not a dispatch authority — EXCEPT cross-story, where the signed wire verb is a precedence-bearing commitment,
not a hint (see STEP 0: a word can be `seeop` here and `op` there, so the foreign verb cannot be re-derived).

## One act, the stamp: the is-be ontology hits the wire

The sharpest cut (Tabor) — and the strongest thing in this doc: the collapse is the **is-be ontology hitting
the wire.** SEE is the **IS** — the inert read of the present noun, *"just is, no act."* The stamp is the
**BE** — the one act, a fact laid. "Render is not an act" is the same blade the whole conversation sharpened:
**the only act is the stamp.** The verb was four labels pretending to be four kinds of acting; underneath
there was only ever read-or-stamp. So the collapse is not an engineering tidy — it is the metaphysics forcing
the protocol into its true shape.

So the honest cardinality is NOT "two acts + the noun." It is **one act — the stamp — bracketed by two
relations:** a **read that feeds it** (see, before) and an **await that pairs it with someone else's stamp**
(call, after). One BE; the rest is IS-and-folding.

- **see** = IS — a read of the present noun, inert (no fact), feeding the act.
- **the stamp** = BE — the one act, a fact on a noun-type (`op`/`beop`/`nameop`).
- **call** = the same stamp **+ an await**. A call's write side IS a fact (a being-targeted stamp); the
  round-trip is your stamp plus an *awaited foreign stamp* — the receiver decides and stamps their OWN, and
  you fold the reply in. The await is not a second act; it is a relation, and it lives in the WIRE (delivery,
  receiver-gate, the await machinery), not the act layer. Call is irreducible AS WIRE, not as an act.

That is where call's specialness actually lives — named precisely: not the ontology (its act is just a fact),
the wire.

**Two "be"s — do not let them touch.** The ontological **BE** here (the act, the stamp, bringing-a-fact-into-
being) is NOT the wire verb `be` (the identity ops). `do` / `be` / `name` are ALL instances of the one
ontological act — each stamps a fact — differing only in the noun: do stamps the **story**, be stamps the
**I**, name stamps a **name**. So "one act" does NOT mean everything becomes the wire-verb `be`; it means
do/be/name were never three kinds of ACTING — they are one act (a stamp) over three nouns. **`do` still exists
— as a wire channel AND as story-facts; so do `be` and `name`.** The verb LABELS all survive (on the wire,
and baked into `fact.verb` in the hash); what collapses is the DISPATCH (route by the word's kind, not a verb
fork). Only SEE is not an act.

**"Inert" means makes-no-fact / no-world-change — NOT no-I/O.** This is the one thing to not misread. A
remote SEE still loads a descriptor, opens a portal, transmits bytes, and runs `authorize()` — real work,
gated work. The IBP protocol SEE (read a descriptor / open a portal remotely) and the in-word noun-read are
the **same thing**: a read that lays no fact. "Inert" is about the chain (no fact, no fold-change), not
about cost (a read is not free).

**See is inert by default — but that is a floor, not a ceiling (Tabor).** The settled claim is structural: a
see is no-act / no-fact — an inert noun. Why see EXISTS is softer (Tabor built render for humans — a person
needs the world drawn before they can act; an LLM being may read the fold directly), but that is motivation,
not a claim that see is "human-only" or disposable — see stays a real channel for any being. And the inert
rule is the floor, not a permanent ceiling: *"maybe after this they will lay."* A see-noun CAN become
fact-laying — but by **composition**, a `do` that eats the noun and stamps (the materialize layer-up), not the
see-primitive growing a fact-tail. So the rule is precise: a see lays no fact *as a see*; laying is always a
`do` that consumes it. The dispatch collapse holds (`kind:seeop` stamps nothing), and the door to "sees that
lay" stays open above it — you compose a do. (This is the same shape as the SEE-residue fix below.)

This is already how a `.word` runs. switch.word reads nouns inertly (`see destination-missing(history)`,
`see being-lives-on(...)`) then makes the ONE act (`Return factParams`). The sees do not count against
one-moment-one-act (presentism, `project_presentism_stamp_invariant`) because they stamp no fact; the fact
is the single act. A moment = N inert noun-reads + ≤1 fact (+ maybe a call). "See and do in one statement"
is not a thing to build — it is what a `.word` already is.

**"Remove the as scaffolding" — LANDED 2026-06-21 (parser fix + the predicate-gate sweep).** Because a see is
a noun (a named thing), a rule references it inline instead of aliasing it through a verb-step. Was: `see
destination-missing(history) as missing` then `If missing, refuse...`. Now: `If destination-missing(history),
refuse...`. The `as $var` was scaffolding for pretending see was a verb-STEP that needs a binding; a predicate
noun is referentially transparent, so the pretense is gone and the noun is referenced where it is used.

The enabling parser surgery (3 changes, all in parser.js / cond.js, unit-covered — verify-cond 31/31):
1. **Paren-aware inline-If hinge.** `splitInlineIf` REPLACES the old regex `/^If (.+?)(?:,(?!and|or)|
   then\b|…)\s+(.+)\.$/`. It scans for the cond/then hinge (a top-level `,` not before and/or, ` then `, or
   `→`/`->`) at paren/brace/bracket/quote depth 0 — so a comma INSIDE a see-op's args (`being-lives-on(caller,
   history)`) never splits cond from consequence. This was the proven blocker: a regex cannot count paren
   depth; a depth-walk (the same shape `splitTopCommas`/`splitInlineEffects` already use for `{}`/`[]`) can.
   `splitInlineEffects` also gained `()` so a multi-arg call in a then-effect is safe.
2. **Inline see-op call as a cond.** A new `seeCall` cond mode (cond.js): `If [not] <op>(args)` runs the
   see-op live through `ctx.env.host[op]({args}, ctx)` — the SAME registry + `{args}` convention the
   evaluator's `callHost` uses for `see <op>(args) as v` — and reads its result's truthiness. parseLeaf lifts
   `name(args)` to `{seeCall, args}`; the gloss-strip was taught to keep call-parens (`op(args)`, no space)
   while still dropping a true gloss (` (not remote)`, whitespace before `(`). Fail-closed if unregistered,
   exactly like `resolvedBy`. The gate fires inline with NO bind, NO fact — the noun's own check IS the gate.
3. **`is less than` / `is greater than`.** Strict ordered compares (compare lt/gt), so the live-object form
   `the hero's health is less than 5` reads the fold AT eval time. (No `under`/`over`/`below` synonyms — they
   surface in prose words.)

The sweep — 18 sites inlined across 8 op `.word`s, every one verifier-gated green: switch.word (3), move.word
(4), portal.word (2), set-world-signal.word (2), truename.word (2), take-able.word (2), ask-able.word (1),
grant-able.word (1), delete-pointer.word (1). Each was a `see <op>(args) as <bind>.` IMMEDIATELY feeding `If
[not|no] <bind>` where `<bind>` was used nowhere else — the pure predicate-gate shape. `If no <bind>` →
`If not <op>(args)` (boolean absence = negation). Full regression: 54 verifiers, every failure pre-existing
(deletepointer/setpointer = a `.histories`-not-bootstrapped gap, proven identical on stock; reducerfold's
`fold coverage`, proven identical; take-able-live 9/9 in isolation — only fails amid 54 back-to-back from
shared-DB contention) — ZERO regressions from the change.

**The principle, pushed all the way (Tabor, 2026-06-21, grounded in `philosophy/rasterize.md`): a seed-type
word IS already a noun, so it should not carry `as` AT ALL.** rasterize names the floor: every fact is a Word
(a past act by a Name); the inner face is a rasterized frame of those word-"pixels"; *"that is all the name
sees (noun type words) and knows what it can do (act next)."* A see-op is one of those noun-type words — a
scribed noun (16.md), not a verb. So `see X as v` was doubly wrong: it spends a verb-STEP and an alias `v` to
re-introduce a thing that already IS a noun referenceable by its own name. The predicate sweep above removed
the `as` exactly where the noun is used once, as a gate. The principle says the rest of the `as`es are the
SAME residue, just harder — and naming where it bottoms out is the honest map:
- **A bind that is reused or field-accessed is a genuine LET, not scaffolding.** `see able-spec-for-grant(able,
  space) as found` then `$found.anchor` ×12: the noun is referenced 12 times and by a sub-field. Inlining it
  means re-running the ancestor-walk 12 times (and needs a call+`.field` expression grammar the evaluator
  lacks). The live-object doctrine (below) says inline = live re-sense, which is RIGHT for a cheap fold-read
  but wrong as a default for a compute-walk. So `as` legitimately survives as a name for a noun whose VALUE is
  reused — that is not the verb-step pretense, it is naming a pixel the next act reads more than once.
- **The real residue is the action-sees — they are not nouns at all (the see=inert violation, already flagged
  here).** `see grant-internal(caller, able, found) as granted`, `see clear-model(...) as cleared`, `see
  write-model(...) as wrote` (model.word — the lines Tabor pointed at): these BIND a discarded result and run
  for their EFFECT. A noun makes no fact; these change the world. Their `as <unused>` is the smell that they
  are `do`s wearing `see`. The clean fix is not "drop the as" but "make them `do`" (or a composed materialize-
  do) — the same blade as the SEE-residue deletion. That is the next pass, and it is a doctrine move (touches
  see=inert), not a mechanical sweep, so it is NOT rushed here.

So the line is precise: `as` is gone for predicate-nouns (done); it stays as a LET for reused/field-accessed
value-nouns (correct, not scaffolding); and the action-sees (`as <unused>`) are mis-typed `see`s whose real
cleanup is becoming `do`s. "They already are nouns" is right — and the ones that still carry `as` and AREN'T
nouns are exactly the bugs the principle surfaces.

**The deeper payoff: programming with living objects (Tabor, 2026-06-21).** Inline + referentially-transparent
means a noun-read is a LIVE sensor, not a snapshot: `if the hero's health < 5` reads the fold AT evaluation
time, so a rule checks the object's CURRENT state. And a noun's PROPERTIES are themselves words — the
possessive `the X's Y` is already in the evaluator (move.word: `the subject's kind equals space`) — so the
conditional vocabulary of a rule is just the object's own properties surfaced as words (`health`, `position`,
`mana` are sub-nouns hanging off the being-noun, each a live quality-read of the fold-projected state). Pair
this with "every act makes a fact" and rules become REACTIVE over living objects: `if X's health < 5, do heal`
re-senses each pass. This is WHY see had to become a noun first — a verb-step (`see X as v` then `if v`)
snapshots once; the inline noun stays live. The whole reactive-programming surface falls out of see-as-noun.

**"Noun auto-becomes matter via a do" is a layer up** — a materialize-op (a do that eats the noun and lays a
create-matter fact), composable and opt-in, never the base. The base stays: a noun is inert until a do eats
it. This is also the honest frame for the SEE residue below: `maybeAutoGrantOnEntry` is a fact riding inside
a render, which the inert-render rule forbids — the grant must move to a do/call the space makes, not a
side-effect of the read.

## The framing question, and its sharp answer

**Where does the read-vs-change distinction live, if not in the verb?**

It MOVES into the word's `kind`. That is the whole collapse in one sentence. Today a caller declares
read-vs-change by choosing a wire verb (`see` vs `do`); the system re-derives the same distinction four
times (one resolver per verb). After the collapse, the WORD carries it once (`kind:seeop` = render/no-act,
`kind in {op,beop,nameop}` = fact), the fold is the single source, and the verb becomes a redundant
cross-check the channel asserts (a do-channel call must resolve to `kind:op`; a wrong-channel call becomes a
clear error instead of a silent mis-stamp).

The sharp consequence: `authorize()`'s `verb` argument should be **derived from the resolved word's kind, at
dispatch**, not taken from the wire — so a caller cannot claim `see` to dodge a fact. The wire verb is then
only the address-contract selector (`envelope.js:108` per-verb address-kind switch) and the transport
short-circuit hint (cross-world forward, see-op skip).

## Where it runs out: CALL's wire, not its act

The collapse is `see | stamp`. CALL's ACT collapses cleanly into a stamp; what does NOT reduce is its WIRE.
Say it without hedging.

**CALL's act is a fact; its irreducible part is the await, which is wire.** A call/summon lays a fact (the
summon record / inbox-stamp IS a being-targeted stamp — one act). A one-way call is therefore *just* a
fact-on-a-being. What does not reduce is the **await**: your stamp paired with an awaited FOREIGN stamp — the
receiver decides and stamps their OWN, and you fold the reply in (`respondMode:"sync"` returns inline; the
`onResponse` closure awaits). That await is not a second kind of act; it is a relation around the one act,
and it lives entirely in the wire — delivery, receiver-gate, the await machinery. So CALL is irreducible **as
wire**, never **as an act**. The earlier "two acts" hedge is wrong: there is one act (the stamp); call adds
an awaited foreign stamp, which is folding, not acting.

The response path drags three load-bearing features that live only at the CALL layer (`call.js`, `SUMMON.md`,
`INTAKE.md`):
- **async cross-being delivery** — envelope + scheduler wake (`scheduler.wake` / `attachHandoff`). A
  fact-word stamps and returns; a call-word stamps AND queues delivery to ANOTHER being's scheduler, then
  awaits. No `kind` routes this.
- **IBPA dual gate** — `permitsReceiverSummon` (`ableAuth.js:390`): the RECEIVER's able decides acceptance by
  `intent`. No render-word or fact-word has a third-party-decides gate. `permitsSummon` carries an
  `as:actor` / `as:receiver` discriminator the other three `permits*` lack.
- **sovereignty** — callers express `intent`, receivers decide (`SUMMON.md`). `intent` is an auth predicate
  separate from the target; `content` is opaque payload. Both checked at the call layer, not at fact-stamp.

Verified hardest fact: **CALL has NO fold-word form today** — no `kind:callword`, no resolver, `call.js`
dispatches directly. It can BECOME a `kind:callword` (so `summon` resolves through the one
`resolveWordFromFold` like the rest), but its execution STRATEGY stays the irreducible
async-delivery / receiver-gate / response path. Fold the lookup; keep the strategy special. That is the
honest endpoint, not "CALL collapses into fact."

**call / recall is Tabor's main worry — handle it last and lightest.** The request (call) and its
response/return leg (recall) are the bidirectional pair, and the response path is exactly the fragile,
irreducible part: the in-memory `onResponse` handoff (`scheduler.js` `state.handoffs`, not serialized), the
`respondMode:"sync"` inline return, the moment-less wire summon (`actId=null`), the cross-world gap. This is
where "fold away extra wiring, never remove logic" is most load-bearing AND most dangerous to get wrong:
STEP 8 folds only the LOOKUP (`summon` becomes a `kind:callword` resolved like any word) and leaves the
entire call/recall STRATEGY verbatim. If a step cannot cleanly separate the lookup-scaffolding from the
response-logic, it stops — call/recall does not get cut to fit the spine; the spine accommodates it. Better
to leave CALL a hand-written verb forever than to flatten the response round-trip into a fire-and-forget
fact.

Further CALL residue the adversarial pass surfaced (all real, all stay wire): the `onResponse` closure lives
in `scheduler.js` `state.handoffs` (in-memory, not serialized — cannot cross the canopy to a foreign story);
the moment-less wire summon has no home-side Act (`actId=null`, asymmetric `crossOrigin`);
`respondMode:"sync"` returns inline and bypasses the inbox projection entirely (the reply is the inner-word
return value, not a delivered call-fact); orientation (forward vs inner-fold) is a sender-determined delivery
directive, gated to self-summons. None of these reduce to fact+projection. (Cross-network act addressing —

## Open questions

- **Does CALL become `kind:callword`, or stay pure wire forever?** Folding the lookup is honest (CALL stops
  being the one verb with zero fold presence) without pretending it collapses into fact. But the async
  scheduler wake + the response round-trip have no kind-routing analog. Where does the receiver-gate live —
  on the binding, or only in the call-tail? The bidirectionality is the load-bearing thing a `kind` cannot
  carry.
- **The SEE residue — RESOLVED, FIXED by deletion 2026-06-21** (not even a CALL — Tabor's sharper cut). A
  space is a noun; it can't act on a being, so there is nothing for the space to do: the auto-grant is just
  DELETED, `seeVerb` is a pure read, and a being self-takes the able from the space's `autoOnEntry` policy via
  its own word + `do:take-able`. No space-being, no space-call, no side-fact hook. `seeop⇒stamps:false` is now
  TRUE in the tree. (See Landed.)
- **Do see-nouns ever lay (via a composed do), and does that composition want first-class grammar?** The base
  is inert (a see stamps nothing) and laying is always a `do` that eats the noun — "maybe after this they will
  lay." Open: how much of that composition deserves an idiom (a `see X then do materialize` shape) vs staying
  ad-hoc, and whether the SEE residue (`maybeAutoGrantOnEntry`) is the FIRST instance of exactly this — a read
  that should knock via a composed do, not a buried side-fact. (The "see is for humans, LLMs read the fold"
  remark is motivation for why see exists, not a structural claim — do not encode see as human-only.)
- **Is NAME a third noun-type under FACT, or its own thing?** `name.js` says NAME is "the identity layer,
  not a world stance" and never resolves a position. As a fact-word its noun is `name` and it folds like any
  other — but the address grammar (no `/`, no `#`) and the I_AM-actor-for-pre-world-ops rule are
  NAME-specific. NAME is NOT a wire verb today. Should the world-facts (declare/banish) become a 6th wire
  verb while connect/release keep the session channel?
- **Cross-story kind authority — FORWARD-LOOKING (no one is federating yet, Tabor).** Not a now-concern; kept
  only so the collapse does not corner itself cross-story later. The shape, for when it lands:
  `runVerbAsForeignActor` takes the sent `verb` (`protocol.js:132`) and the deed is signed over it
  (`crossWorld.js:269`), so a foreign story trusts the SENT verb (it is in the signed hash; re-deriving kind

- **Does the chain eventually carry `kind` directly instead of a kind-derived `verb` shadow?** Facts are
