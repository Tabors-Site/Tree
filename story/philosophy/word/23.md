# 23 — One word, one commit, one fact: the skipAudit cleanup (the fold does the rest)

> **THE SPACEBAR LAW (Tabor, definitive 2026-06-22 — settles the re-litigation for good).** A commit — *the space* — lays **one fact**. Letters are IS-side: they accumulate and lay nothing; the space is the BE, and it commits the one fact. So **one word = one commit = one fact.** A word's *compositeness* (break+fast, create+space+named+X) is its **content — the letters of it — NOT a count of facts**; the definition unfolds IS-side to fill that one fact's content, it does not litter the chain with N. create-matter is the proof: all the resolve-spec machinery, and "the lone world write" is **one** birth fact. (I briefly "corrected" this to "one act → N facts / ΔF" after a judge panel over-read math.md's `a(Φ)=ΔF` — that was wrong; `ΔF` being multi-*reel* for one deed is not "N words, one act." Reverted.)
>
> **The intrinsic/separable knife IS the spacebar — no entailment test, no guardrail.** You hit space where the word *ends*. "create space named X" isn't *done* without the name (a word with a letter missing) → name is **inside**, one fact. "create space X" **is** done before owner (a separate complete thought) → owner is the **next space, the next word, the next fact, the NEXT MOMENT.** The fat-spec is a **run-on** — words crammed with the spaces deleted; you don't catch it with a test, you catch it by hitting space where the word ends. Genesis is a **sequence of moments** (4-stamped.js), each one word / one commit / one fact. **Facts = spaces.**

**TL;DR:** `skipAudit` is drift, not a feature. The clean shape: an op returns **`stampsFact(result, params, target)`**, the dispatcher commits the **one** fact, the **reducer folds the derivable consequences**, and anything that's a *separate complete thought* (owner after create, heaven after create) is its **own op in its own moment** (genesis runs them as a sequence). Every `skipAudit` op is one of: *collapse to one fact + fold*, *decompose the run-on into separate moments*, or *recognize the real verb* — cleaning each drops `skipAudit` until the flag has zero users and is deleted.

Successor to 17.md (the verb collapses into the word) and 22.md (the false shape of objects). This doc is the doctrine + the living cleanup tracker; **COMPLETE** is written at the very bottom when the sweep finishes.

## The realization

### 1. The law is already written; `skipAudit` contradicts it
`factResult.js:8-11`: *"EVERY ACT MAKES A FACT: the dispatcher stamps unconditionally… There is no 'lay no fact'; there is only 'lay a fact that records the act and folds nothing.'"* `skipAudit:true` is the escape hatch that opts an op out of that one stamper. It is the residue of an older pattern, not a design.

### 2. Why the double-stamp risk exists (what's behind them)
Every `skipAudit` op has a **helper that stamps its own fact**, so the dispatcher's auto-Fact would land a second one and the op silences it. `setOwner` → an inner `set-space` fact; `setStoryConfigValue` → a `name`-act on the library reel; `deleteMatterAndFile` → two `set-matter` facts. The inversion: **the helper stamps by hand instead of returning the stamp for the one dispatcher.**

### 3. The clean shape is already built and named
`stampsFact(result, params, target)` (factResult.js:24) sets `_factParams`/`_factTarget`; the do.js auto-Fact reads them and stamps the **one** fact. `skipAudit` ops are exactly the ones that *don't* return `stampsFact`. P0 already proved the move at the identity floor: `writeBeFact`/`writeNameFact` were self-stamping helpers (connect/name); deleting them + the keystone (`emitWordFact`) made the **dispatcher** stamp the one fact. Cleaning a do-op's `skipAudit` is the same move.

### 4. One act = one fact. The entailment lives in the FOLD.
22.md is literal: *"the do does exactly one thing — it lays a fact"; "one act, one change. The rest is the fold."* An act's entailed consequences are **computed by the reducer**, not hand-stamped as extra facts. A `stampsFacts` (one act → N facts) idea was raised and **rejected**: one Word/token mapping to N stamps is the multi-field object sneaking back **at the act layer** instead of the param bag — it breaks *one word = one stamp*, the invariant the whole model rests on. **Keep `stampsFact` singular.**

### 5. The false shape (22.md), and the knife
A multi-field object isn't in the reel — there's no struct holding N slots. There are N past `do`s, each setting one thing; the object is **what they fold to** (a Datomic datom stream: `[entity, attribute, value]`). **Identity is never a field** — for a being it's the **anchor** (the genesis/birth stamp every attribute-`do` chains to), for matter it's the **hash**. The knife is **intrinsic vs separable**: a *parameter* is what makes the act *that* act (create-space needs its name; move needs its coord); anything *separable* (state that can change on its own later) is its **own** act that folds into the entity. A fat parameter bag is the struct in the act's clothes.

### 6. The three shapes under `skipAudit`
- **Collapse to one fact + fold.** The op stamps several facts that are one entity's consequences. Replace with **one** fact (a tombstone/marker, or the irreducible change) and let the **reducer derive** the rest. *Example:* `deleteMatterAndFile` (matters.js:597-609) stamps two `set-matter` facts — both `of:{kind:"matter"}`, **same reel**, two fields (`spaceId=DELETED`, `beingId=DELETED`) — a 2-field-object false shape. Clean form: one `do:end-matter` fact; the matter reducer folds *an ended matter reads absent-from-space and unheld*.
- **Decompose into many acts.** Genuinely separable state crammed into one op. *Example:* `merge-histories` emits N reset facts in a loop (N acts behind one name); the fat `create-*` specs cram coords/able/owner into the birth stamp (those are separable → their own folded `do`s; only name + lineage are intrinsic to birth).
- **Recognize the real verb.** It isn't a `do` at all. *Example:* `set-config`/`delete-config` open `withNameAct` and stamp `verb:"name"` on the library reel — they're `name`-acts; the do-op shell dissolves.

### 7. Findings that ground it
- `deleteMatterAndFile` is **already** a pure fold-act: **no blob delete** (*"bytes are content-addressed and possibly shared by other matter (dedup); the retention sweeper `casSweep` and purge-content own blob lifecycle"*). `casSweep` is the reference-collector reclaiming at refcount-zero — exactly what `matter = hash` implies. **`AndFile` is a dead fossil name**; the rm was welded in only when JS owned the file 1:1, and content-addressing already dissolved that.
- The **effect-class membrane** (pure fold / effectful floor, fire-once-skip-on-replay) lives inside a verb's body **only when it has a real host effect**. End-matter has none (the blob isn't touched). When a verb does have one (a real `rm`, a network send), the floor half is tagged effectful — the same tag the matter-backed words use (P5).

### 8. The one genuinely-open question (NOT `stampsFacts`)
End-matter collapses trivially because it's **same-reel** — the matter reducer reads the matter's own "ended" fact and derives its own state. A genuinely **cross-reel** act ("B takes M": one fact on B's reel, M's holder is a consequence) needs the fold to do a **reverse-index / cross-reel lookup** (Datomic's reverse-ref). If the fold has that, *nothing ever needs more than one fact* and `stampsFacts` is dead for good. That's a **fold capability** (engine lane), needed only when the first take-style op arrives — not for any same-reel op.
