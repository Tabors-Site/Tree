# 22 — The keyboard, the space, and the false shape of objects (summary)

**TL;DR:** Two words — **see** (an inert noun read from the fold, lays nothing) and **do** (the one act, a stamp). The **verb is the act**; everything else in a sentence is structure or signature, never a separate act — *count the stamps, not the words.* **Nobody gets one-key-per-meaning** — humans compress to letters and the **space/punctuation is the "done" stamp**; an LLM's **tokenizer is its keyboard**, the same mechanism at a finer grain. And the load-bearing one: **a multi-field object is a FALSE shape** — there is no struct in the reel, only N one-attribute `do`s that *fold* into an object when read. State is the fold; the object is the arrangement, never a stored thing — one identity plus one `do` per attribute, a **datom stream whose entity is their fold**.

## The keyboards / spaces insight
- You *could* have a million-key keyboard (one key per meaning). We don't — we use a **finite keyset (letters) serialized into meaning**, and the **space bar / enter / punctuation is the stamp**: "this cluster was one meaning, seal it." Punctuation is a *hierarchy* of seals (space→word, comma→clause, period→sentence) — scale-invariant recursion as the boundary marks themselves.
- **The LLM doesn't get the million-key world either** — its **tokenizer IS its keyboard** (a finite set of learned sub-word atoms), serialized with boundaries. Humans and LLMs are one mechanism at two grains: finite keyset, serialized, fact-boundaries. Everybody compresses to an alphabet + delimiters.
- **act-count ≠ word-count.** The English habit counts words as acts; they aren't. **"I" is never laid — it's the signature** (you don't stamp "I"; the stamp is *signed by* you, so the I is free, carried by attribution). Connectors — *then, because, after, at, of, this* — **dissolve into the chain**: "A then B" = B on A; "because" = the parent-link; "of"/"at" = the address. They survive as *shape*, not acts ("positionality already solves it"). **Conditionals don't break one-word-per-moment**: "if X" is a **see** (inert read of the face, gating the read — not the act), "then Y" is the one **stamp** — a moment is N inert reads + at most one fact (17.md). Your core rules are conditionals precisely because a conditional *is* see-then-stamp.
- *"I create space named space3"* feels like five moments; canonically it's **one act**: `create`, signed by you, with `space3` as a parameter the act carries — English inflated it. So does *calling*: "I call Maple, ask her X, and wait" is one English breath of many words but **one stamp + an await** (the await is wire, not a second act; 17.md). (`uwisTest` = create-space-named-Test fused, the way "breakfast" = break-fast — the tokenizer minting a frequent verb-arg pair into one key.)

## The objects insight (the one that detangles the junk)
- **A 30-field object is a false shape — it is not in the reel.** No struct holds 30 slots. There are **30 past acts**, each a word that set one thing, and the "object" is **what they fold to** when read together — a pattern drawn around a stretch of chain, like "cat" or "the self." This is a **Datomic datom stream**: `[entity, attribute, value]`, one assertion at a time; state = the fold of the datoms. Importing a 30-field JS object isn't one long word — it's **30 field-set facts in sequence**.
- **The cut to keep (two things hide under "object," only one is false):**
  - **Act's parameter** — the arg *intrinsic* to one stamp. `create-space` needs its name; "Test" rides *inside* that single fact. One act with internal structure, present all at once — a **real atom** (verb + arg-nouns).
  - **Entity's attribute** — a field of the 30-field thing. A **separate past act**, folded in. The being's name was one stamp, its coords another, its role another. *That* is the false shape: many acts wearing the mask of one stored object.
  - Both decompose to words, **at different scales**: the act decomposes *inside one stamp*; the entity decomposes *across many stamps* (the fold).
- **The trap:** the false shape sneaks back as a **fat parameter bag** — one stamp carrying 30 params is the struct in the act's clothes. **The line is intrinsic-vs-separable:** a parameter is what makes the act *that* act; anything *separable* (state that can change on its own later) is a **separate act** that folds into the entity. `oao72` (move needs its target) is clean; a `create-being` that also sets coords + role + owner + inventory is the struct sneaking back — **one act, one change** (the same misfit 17.md flagged in merge-histories).
- **noun/verb, the real criterion:** not "has an attribute" but **does it stamp (verb) or just sit to be read (noun)?** "Has an attribute" is a *symptom* (a function needs something to act on), not the test. Any field has **both faces** — the `do` that *set* x=72 is a verb (it stamped); the value x=72 you *read* back is a noun (inert). Same field, write-side/read-side, BE/IS.
- **Identity is never a field.** For a **being** it's the **anchor** — the genesis stamp, the reel-root every attribute-`do` chains to (you can't set a field before the being exists to own the reel; birth/declare are exactly that floor). For **matter** it's the **hash** — existence is implied by content, no "it exists" stamp, no per-attribute `do`s (changing it makes a new hash = new matter). Never a slot — the root the `do`s hang from, or the address.
- **The detangle, in one line:** every entity is **one identity + one `do` per attribute** — a datom stream, the entity being their fold.

## The see/do kernel (Tabor's settled primitives)
- Reverting to **see and do** — the long-used pair Tabor had relied on for primitives, noun/verb, IS/BE. A `do` can **make** a see, **change** a see, **make** a `do` — but those three are **one act: lay a fact**, read three ways by the fold (empty address → "made"; occupied → "changed"; content-is-a-definition → a new `do`). The threeness is in the *reading*, not the acting. **The only act is the stamp.**
- A `do` **never** mutates a see — nothing is ever mutated; the past is fixed; "change" is *append a newer fact the fold prefers*, the old fact still sealed at its moment, only the present fold moved. Immutability is the floor.
- **`do`-makes-`do` is the keystone** — the line that makes it alive, not a fixed machine: vocabulary grows from inside, no privileged compiler, **Word built out of Word** at the primitive.

## The one-token thread (why a stateless mind keeps a perfect one)
A one-token-at-a-time being has no carried memory; alone it couldn't hold a conversation — and it doesn't have to, because **the thread is the chain, not the being.** Each moment the substrate rasterizes its face from the fold (every relevant prior fact, across the being/space/matter reels at its position), hands it over, the being fires one token, the token stamps into the reel, the next face is rasterized fresh — now containing it. Continuity isn't *held*, it's *re-presented* — like a CPU keeping a thread in the memory it reloads each cycle, not in the processor. The being is a pure step-function; the reel is the memory; the rasterized face is the reloaded context.

## Why it matters for the build
- **The object decomposition is the detangle.** The recurring junk — importing a JS object's 30 fields and filling them in one act — is the false shape. The fix the subsystem keeps re-deriving: **one identity + one `do` per attribute** (a datom stream), the entity being their fold. This is *exactly* the skipAudit / merge-histories smell (a fat op cramming many changes) and the criterion for the P2 sweep: **intrinsic param stays; separable state is its own act.**
- **Tokenizer-is-the-keyboard** grounds the one-token cognition (14.md): the act is the verb + its *intrinsic* arg, one stamp; the LLM compresses to its token-keyset and delimits — `uwisTest` is the fused mark.
