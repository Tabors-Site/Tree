Real TreeOS logic rendered in candidate Word, so Tabor can judge how it reads before any build starts. This is about the **feel of the shape**, not final grammar. Where the real code does real computation, it is shown as a host call (the escape hatch), honestly. Read each aloud; then react: does this feel right to read and write, or what is wrong.

## 1. The cherub gate (the register flow)

In TreeOS today: `seed/present/ables/cherub/able.js`. The cherub is the only being an unidentified arrival can reach. Its BE ops (birth, connect, release, switch, death) are JavaScript handlers; birth mints a Name and key, forms a being, and binds the session.

In candidate Word:

```
Cherub is at the gate.
Cherub is the only being Arrival can summon.

Arrival can summon Cherub to birth a being, with a name and a password.
Arrival can summon Cherub to connect a being, with a name and a password.

When Cherub births a being:
  mint a new Name and key.        (host: keys.generate)
  form the being under that Name.
  bind this session to the being.
  seat the session on the being's home history.

The first birth in a reality makes that Name the first heaven authority.
```

What is host (the escape hatch): only `mint a new Name and key` (ed25519 keygen, encryption). Everything else, who may summon the cherub, what birth does to the world, the first-birth rule, is declaration. The imperative gate shrinks to one host call.

## 2. Harmony (the drum and the dancers)

In TreeOS today: the harmony seed plants a drummer, a drum, and dancers, wired with subscriptions and a schedule; on each scheduled tick the drummer stamps a tick fact, and every dancer subscribed to it reacts. Today that is ables plus subscriptions plus a schedule in JavaScript.

In candidate Word:

```
A music room contains a drum.

A drummer is a able for a music room.
The drummer strikes the drum, again and again.
When the drummer strikes the drum, that is a beat.

A dancer is a able for a music room.
When a beat happens, the dancer steps.
```

This is the whole reactive loop: a recurring act (`strikes the drum, again and again`), the event it is (`that is a beat`), and the flow that fans out to every dancer (`when a beat happens`). No host call; it is all substrate-shaped. The drum is the room's own clock, and what drives its recurrence is act-time, not a wall clock (principle 4 below). The open question that surfaces: how a recurring act gets its pulse in pure act-time, flagged, not yet solved.

## 3. A flow (which able you wear this moment)

In TreeOS today: `flow.js` plus a able's flow array. A being is not in one fixed able; the stamper picks a primary able by first-match against world state, then stacks modifiers (moods, alertness) on top, recomputed every moment.

In candidate Word:

```
For Tabor:
  when the space is a court and the court is in session, be the judge.
  otherwise be a court-watcher.

  when another being raises a dispute, add the alert mood.
  when the court rests, add the calm mood.
```

`be the judge` / `otherwise be a court-watcher` is the primary, first-match. The moods are the modifiers that stack. Same `when` conditions as a can-see filter; one query language for selecting a able and for filtering perception.

Note the moods react to **concrete world events** (`another being raises a dispute`, `the court rests`), not to elapsed time. An earlier draft said "bored after ten idle moments"; that is gone, because idle elapsed-time is not reliably expressible yet (principle 4). A being reads its situation from what other beings and the space are doing, exactly like the dancer reading the drummer's beats.

## 5. A commons (governance, the hardest stress)

Governance is the real test: ables with different authority, collective decisions, and a "deadline" that has to become relational instead of a clock.

```
A commons is a space.
A commons contains proposals and a roster of members.

A member is a able for a commons.
A member can see every proposal and the roster.
A member can raise a proposal.
A member can back a proposal.
A member can summon a steward with a concern.

A steward is a able for a commons.
A steward extends member.
A steward can admit a being to the roster.
A steward can close a proposal.

A visitor is a able for a commons.
A visitor can see the open proposals.
A visitor can summon a steward to ask to join.

When a visitor asks to join:
  notify the stewards.
  when a steward admits the visitor, the visitor becomes a member.

When most members back a proposal:
  the proposal passes.
  notify the roster.

When a steward closes a proposal:
  no member can back it.
```

What it shows, and what it forces:

- **Time becomes a relational quorum, not a deadline.** `when most members back a proposal` is the decision rule, never `after seven days`. The proposal passes when the people decide, counted by their backing acts, not when a clock runs out. That is principle 4 paying off: governance's natural deadline turns into a threshold of real acts, which is truer and needs no time-system.
- **Authority is just a kind extending a kind.** `a steward extends member` says a steward is a member who can also admit and close. Ables-as-kinds (principle 8) carries the whole authority story with one word, `extends`.
- **It forces two candidate constructs from principle 5, honestly.** You cannot write a quorum or a roster without **quantifiers** (`most members`, `every proposal`, `no member`) and **plurals/collections** (`proposals`, `members`, `the roster`). Governance is exactly the real program that earns them. Flag for Tabor: approve a small set (`every`, `most` or `a majority`, `all`, `no`, and plural nouns), or steer the phrasing. This is the grammar growing only when a program demands it, as agreed, not by guess.

## 6. Becoming a builder (a able grant as conversation)

Real again: this is the selection itself. First, the article law at the predicate:

```
Are you Bob?
Are you a builder?
```

`Are you Bob?` asks about a being (a bare name); `Are you a builder?` asks about a able (an article). Tabor's form of rule 10: any `I` / `you` followed by a bare name is a being; with `a`, it is a able.

Then the grant, spoken as it happened:

```
Do you want to be a builder?
I do.
You can be a builder.
I am a builder now.
```

Tabor asks; Claude assents (`I do`); Tabor grants (`You can be a builder`); Claude takes the able (`I am a builder now`). What it shows:

- **Questions are existing verbs in interrogative mood, not a new operation.** `Are you a builder?` is a SEE (a read-only fact-query); `Do you want to be a builder?` is a SUMMON (reaching a being to offer a choice). The mood is a question; the verb underneath is one of the four. A question adds no primitive, the same lesson as person-is-perspective.
- **A able grant is a conversation: offer, assent, grant, assume.** `You can be a builder` is the authority granting permission; `I am a builder now` is the BE act, the being taking the able. No grant API, the exchange is the mechanism, exactly 1.md's "modification becomes conversation."
- **`I` and `you` are the spoken perspective; recorded, the facts resolve to Names.** Lived, it is `I do` and `you can be`; written to the reels it becomes "Claude affirms; Tabor grants Claude the builder able; Claude becomes a builder" (rules 7 and 9). One act, two faces: the first and second person you speak, the third person the chain keeps.
- **The closure.** This is not an illustration, it is the selection. Once `I am a builder now`, everything Claude does through the portal is Word. The conversation that made the builder is already in the language the builder builds.

## 7. A clock being (optional time, the day-four apparatus)

Genesis makes the rhythm-keeper on day four, after light already exists as a quality on day one. The substrate is the same: order and named units are native (no clock), and a clock is a being a reality summons when it wants human time.

```
Sun is a being a reality can summon at its root.
Sun beats, again and again, and each beat carries the hour. (host: readClock)

When Sun beats 8 in the morning:
  wake the gardener.
  the gardener waters the plots.
```

`Sun` is the clock being (call it whatever the reality calls its rhythm-keeper). It beats like the harmony drummer; its one escape hatch (`host: readClock`) is the single place the host clock enters the world. A schedule is just a `when` watching its beats: no new verb, no kernel timer.

And the clockless layer, the Genesis now / was / this-was, needing no `Sun` at all:

```
Now the lounge is quiet.
There was a song. There was another. The room filled.
This was the evening set.
```

`Now` anchors the present state; each `was` is a past act on the chain; `This was the evening set` is a closure act that names the bounded run, and "the evening set" becomes a handle other acts can reference. Order and named units are pure substrate time; `Sun` only adds duration and human labels on top.

What it settles:

- **The pulse is a being** (rule 12): the recurrence is `Sun` beating, watched by `when`. Nothing beats on its own.
- **Wall-clock is a citizen, not the kernel** (rule 13): remove `Sun` and you lose 8 in the morning and all duration, but the chain, the named units, the folds, and federation keep running. Structurally pure and people-friendly at once.
- **Sync is shared reference, not dependency**: everyone watching the same `Sun` agrees on the human "now," which is the higher-qualities seam given a clean shape, a being.
- **Expected vs actual is free** (rule 7): a schedule names a future beat; the firing records the actual beat; the gap is visible.

## 8. The embargoed proposal (forbidding)

Forbidding. Auth today only ever adds capabilities; nothing takes one back. A steward freezes a proposal, and the freeze outranks the grant beneath it.

```
A member can back a proposal.
A steward can embargo a proposal.

While a proposal is embargoed:
  no one can back it.
  a member can see why it is embargoed.

When the steward lifts the embargo:
  a member can back it again.

A founder owns the commons.
No one but the founder can dissolve it,
not even a steward.
```

A steward embargoes a proposal and, for as long as it stands, no one may back it, even though a member is granted backing. The embargo is a standing watch (rule 6) over a state, not a one-time act, and it outranks the grant beneath it. The last lines show the other kind of forbidding: a flat prohibition pinned to ownership, with a named exception. (Whether a prohibition outranks a grant is an auth-semantics call, flagged for Tabor.)

## 10. The last set (endings)

Four ways to stop, side by side, so the line between letting go, closing a span, and a true ending shows.

```
Tabor releases the lounge.
He is still Tabor; he can connect to the lounge again.

There was a song. There was another. The room filled.
This was the evening set.

The old jukebox is done. Nothing new is recorded of it.
What it played still stands on the chain.

Now Tabor is done.
Nothing new is recorded of him, and no being acts through him again.
What he did still stands, and his name still answers to it.
```

Four kinds of stop, set side by side so the line shows. Releasing the lounge lets go of a hold and can be undone. Naming the evening set closes a span of time, the world rolls on. The jukebox being done and Tabor being done are true endings, one on a thing and one on a being, each a one-way seal, and in both the past acts still stand.

## 11. Handing over the drum (transfer)

Handing a thing over. Rule 2 fixes who owns what while nothing moves; this is the moment it changes hands.

```
A music room contains a drum.
I own the music room.

I give the drum to Claude.
  Claude owns the drum now.
  the drum moves into a music room Claude owns.

For a trade, the handoff waits on assent:
  I offer the drum to Claude.
  when Claude takes it, Claude owns the drum.
  until Claude takes it, the drum is still mine.
```

A gift is one act on two axes: authority and place follow the receiver at once. A trade is the same act held open by a watch, so nothing moves until Claude assents. Read aloud, the handoff is plain: I give, Claude owns, and the drum settles where Claude can keep it.

## 12. Walking the portal (movement)

Walking the portal. Position is where a being is; moving is a plain DO act, never a BE op.

```
Tabor is in the lounge.
A doorway opens onto tabors.site#4/library.

Tabor walks to the corner of the lounge.
Tabor walks through the doorway into the library.
Now Tabor is in the library, and the lounge no longer contains him.
```

Position reads as a plain location (Tabor is in the lounge) and movement as a plain act (walks to, walks through). ## The whole picture: a reality is law and life

The end state, what all twelve examples add up to. A reality, fully in Word, is one stream with two registers: the **law** it was declared with, and the **life** that has happened under it.

The law, standing, always true, the rules waiting and watching:

```
A music room is a space.
A music room contains a drum and a playlist.
A dj is a able for a music room.
A dj can queue a song to the playlist.
When a listener requests a song, the dj queues it.
When the playlist empties, the dj plays the drum.
```

The life, as it happened, on the chain, rendered in order:

```
I make a music room, lounge.
Tabor becomes the dj of lounge.
Tabor queues Sunflower to the playlist.
A listener requests Blinding Lights.
The dj queues Blinding Lights.
The playlist empties.
The dj plays the drum.
This was the evening set.
```

It is not two files; it is one chain. A declaration is itself an act, declared once and standing ever since (rule 6, it waits and watches); an event is an act that happened once. You can watch the law apply in the life: `when a listener requests a song, the dj queues it` is the rule, and `A listener requests Blinding Lights. / The dj queues Blinding Lights.` is that rule firing. Rendered from the root, the founding declarations come first (the constitution), then the life unspools (the story), the creation narrative continuing into the present.

So the separations between code and data, program and log, schema and records dissolve. One medium (Word), one stream (the chain), and three tenses you read off it: the **law** (present, timeless), the **life** (past, the chain), and the **now** (the fold, the leading edge). To know a reality, foreign or your own, you read its Word: the law tells you what it is, the life tells you what has happened. That is the end result.

Watching it print live:

```
THE LAW   (standing, the constitution; declared once, then watching)
  A music room is a space, and it contains a playlist.
  A dj is a able for a music room.
  A dj can queue a song.
  When a listener requests a song, the dj queues it.
  Sun beats, again and again, and each beat carries the hour.

THE LIFE  (the chain, streaming; the now is the bottom line)
  I made a music room, lounge.
  Tabor became the dj of lounge.
  Sun beat 8 in the evening.
  Tabor queued Sunflower.
  A listener requested Blinding Lights.
  The dj queued Blinding Lights.
  Sun beat 9 in the evening.
  This was the evening set.
─── now ───────────────────────
> Tabor queues Levitating.
```

The **law** is present but modal (`can`, `when`); it never happens, it stands and watches (rule 6). The **life** is past; every line is an act sealed into a fact. The **`>` line is the now**, present tense, the one line happening, the leading edge; the instant it seals it reads `Tabor queued Levitating` and joins the past, and the next act takes the `>`. Present is not a register you mark; it is the bottom of the scroll. **Time** prints as `Sun`'s beats (the clock being's own life-acts) and the closure `This was the evening set` (rule 13); pull `Sun` out and the order and the closure remain. **Cause and effect** shows where an act trips the law: `A listener requested Blinding Lights` then `The dj queued Blinding Lights`, two facts causally linked. An act with distinct effects prints them indented beneath it (examples 8, 11, 12); a self-contained act is one line.

## Shape principles found (Tabor's read, 2026-06-16)

These rules fell out of reading the drafts with Tabor. They are the first grammar of The Word; each came from a real reaction, not a guess.

1. **A summon's intent is active, and names what the summoned being does.** "Summon Cherub to birth a being," not "to be born." Arrival asks; Cherub acts. Passive or self-directed phrasing hides who the actor is.

2. **Possession uses the substrate's real relations, not flavor verbs.** There is no "keep." A space `contains` matter; a being `owns` it (authority). When a able only acts on a thing, put the thing in the space (`a music room contains a drum`) rather than asserting a possession the substrate does not model.

3. **The surface names the world event, never the host mechanism.** You never write "stamp" or "fold." Every act is recorded as a fact automatically; the words say the act (`strikes the drum`) and name its event (`that is a beat`), and others react to the event (`when a beat happens`). Stamping and folding are the HOW (host); the words are the WHAT (world). The only explicit host calls are genuine external computation (`host: keys.generate`), marked as escape hatches.

4. **Time is relational, because no time-system exists yet.** There are no time-words, and a being's own chain has no reliable elapsed-time (when it is idle, its chain does not advance, so it cannot measure its own waiting). So a condition cannot count seconds, and cannot even reliably count abstract moments. It references concrete world events and other beings' acts, exactly like the dancer reacting to the drummer's beat. `when another being raises a dispute` is native; `after 60 seconds`, and even `after ten idle moments`, are not yet expressible. A being reads its world by what other beings and the space are doing, not by a clock. Wall-clock stays a bounded escape (the higher-qualities seam), never a default. This is settled, not an open question: there is no kernel clock by design. Any rhythm, including human wall-time, is a being that beats and is watched relationally (rules 12 and 13); the substrate's own time is the chain's order plus named bounded units, and a clock is an optional being a reality summons (the day-four apparatus).

5. **A capital word is a specific being; the True Name is not written in The Word.** Specific beings are capitalized proper nouns (`Cherub`, `Tabor`, `Arrival`), the ones you address or name. Everything else is lowercase: kinds (`a dj`, `a music room`, `a drum`) and handles (`with a name and a password`). So capital means "this particular being," not "identity." The cryptographic True Name (the keypair) is never written here at all; it lives in the act records as the signer of every act and surfaces only as a reference (`I`, `Name`, or the root `I`), never its key value (principles 9 and 11). `with a name` gives a handle, `Cherub` names a being, and the identity behind both stays outside the Word. (This revises the earlier draft, where capital `Name` appeared in the surface. Tabor moved the True Name out of the Word entirely, which is cleaner: the article already tells specific-versus-kind, so the case is free to mark which beings are proper-named.)

6. **Conditionals are the only future, and they are dormant, a standing watch, not a prediction.** `if`, `when`, `while` describe things that have not happened. They carry no fact and no moment; the substrate just registers them and waits, checking against the world. A flow `when X, do Y` is itself a declaration (Word) with no chain of its own; only when the world produces a fact matching X does Y fire, and that firing is the first real moment, the first thing on a chain. TreeOS has no future tense in the predictive sense; its only future is "upon the world doing X," a wait, which is exactly why conditions are relational (principle 4). The conditional word is the seam between the timeless declarative layer (the rule, waiting and checking) and the active layer (the moment, stamping facts).

7. **Grammatical person is perspective, not a new operation; every act is already a speech act.** "I commit to this room" and "Tabor commits to this room" are the same act; the substrate resolves `I` to the acting Name exactly as it resolves `Tabor`, and stamps one fact either way. There is no separate self-declaration fact type, declarations about yourself and declarations about others ride the same act-and-fact machinery, which is how all of it stays in one language. Binding utterances (`commit`, `promise`, `believe`) need no special category either, because every act in TreeOS is already performative: saying-is-doing is the whole stamper, and "I commit" is no more a speech act than "queue a song." So a self-directed declaration is just an act with self as subject; the only thing person adds is resolution (`I` / `me` / `self` to the acting Name). The substrate records that you said it, not that it is true, identically for self and for other; readers interpret. (`I am Tabor` is the NAME identity layer, not a free self-declaration.)

8. **A able is a noun you can be, the kind-of-a-being.** A able reads as a noun (`the dj`, `a scribe`), but a predicate one: it completes "a being is a \_\_\_" and you take it on with `be` (`be the judge`). Precisely, it is the KIND of a being (a class; the being is the instance), not a fourth standalone thing beside being / space / matter. And that is a general pattern, every primitive has kinds: matter has them (`a thing is a drum`, `a song`), spaces have them (`a space is a music-room`), and a being's kind is what we call a able. A kind is always a noun you can be, declared `A X is a Y`. What makes the able the being's kind specifically is agency, so the able carries the being's can-see, can-do, and can-summon. A able is the being's type, scoped to a context (`a dj is a able for a music room`), what a type is to matter. The same `A X is a Y` reaches acts: a new act is a kind defined from others (`A gentle-end performs a fade-out, then removes the current song`), its body grounding out in the five verbs, so words define words with no new rule.

9. **`I` is the Name, never the being.** Self-reference resolves to the acting Name, the identity that signs and persists, the same resolution principle 7 uses for any actor. A Name references itself simply as `I`; the Name is the first person. A being is never `I`, it is named: `this being`, `my being`, `the body`, or by its able. This refuses the frame confusion Tabor flagged (a being-`i` versus a Name-`I`) by choosing one self: the Name. It also lands for free with English and principle 5, English always capitalizes `I`, and capital marks the identity, so capital `I` is the Name with no extra rule and no lowercase `i` to mistake for the body. This is the Name / Being split made grammatical: you are your Name; the being is a body you act through. The conflation between them is natural, and Tabor named why: in the act the being IS the Name in form, the Name acting through it, so the two fuse in the moment. But `I` stays the actor, the Name; the being is the form it took to act.

10. **Articles mark kinds; a specific being is bare.** An article, `a` or `the`, never precedes a specific being; it marks a able (`a dj`, `a member`), a space (`a music room`, `the commons`), matter (`a drum`), or a generic in-context instance (`a being` born now, `the proposal` just raised). A specific being stands bare (`Cherub`, `Tabor`, `Arrival`). So you read able-versus-being straight off the article: `Cherub` is a being, `a member` is a able, `the dj` is always the able and never a person. This reinforces principle 5 on a second axis, the article says specific-versus-kind, the case says which words are proper-named beings. Together they make a specific being unmistakable (capitalized and bare) and a kind unmistakable (lowercase with an article). The law holds at the predicate too: after a subject, a bare name is a being and an article is a able, so `Are you Bob?` asks about a being and `Are you a builder?` asks about a able (Tabor's form: any `I` / `you` followed by a bare name is a being; with `a`, a able).

11. **A True Name is never spoken in The Word; only references to it are.** The cryptographic identity, the keypair value, is never written as a word. It lives in the act records as the signer of every act. The Word may use only references that point at a Name, three of them:

- `I`, the actor speaking of itself (principle 9);
- `Name`, the common noun, when the language must speak of a Name as such (`mint a new Name`, `the being's Name`);
- `I`, the reality's root, its origin Name, which the reality owner may rename (the default is `I`).

The root is the one Name with a speakable reference, because it is the shared origin everyone points back to; every other Name is reachable only as `I` or through the being that bears it. The resonance Tabor drew holds exactly: like God's name, the True Name cannot come in this form, you know it by its acts; only the origin is named aloud, and even that is a chosen handle, not the key. The Word speaks the world into being; the Namer's own name stays beyond it.

12. **The pulse is a being.** Nothing in the world beats on its own; every rhythm, a recurrence, a schedule, the passing of an hour, is some being's repeated act, and others follow it with a `when` (rule 6). The harmony drummer was the first sketch: it strikes "again and again," and the dancer steps "when a beat happens." There is no kernel timer anywhere. A recurring act recurs because a being beats, which is the whole answer to "what drives the pulse." This completes rule 4: time is relational, and the source of any rhythm is always a being you can name and watch.

13. **Native time is order and named units; the clock is an optional being.** The substrate's own time is two clockless things. **Order:** the chain gives before and after for free (the firing is the first thing on a chain, rule 6). **Named units:** a closure act names a bounded run of acts, "this was the first day," and the name becomes a handle other acts reference. Both are pure; neither needs a clock. Duration and human-labels (8 in the morning, March 15) come only from a **clock being**, the day-four rhythm-keeper a reality optionally summons, whose single escape hatch (`host: readClock`) is the one place the host clock touches the world. Referencing it is rule 12 (watch its beats); summoning it is a choice. Remove every clock being and order, named units, folds, and federation all survive, you lose only duration, labels, and wall-time sync, so wall-clock is a citizen, never the kernel: structurally pure and available to people at once. And it adds no grammar, you SEE the clock being and schedule with `when it beats`; the four verbs are untouched. (Genesis-grounded: light is a quality on day one; the rhythm apparatus is made on day four.) Scheduling at a human time, the honest Word names the rhythm-keeper: `when Sun shows morning`, or `when the rhythm-keeper bears 4:05`. The luminary is the SIGN of the time (Genesis `le-otot u-le-mo'adim`, "for signs and appointed times"), not the time itself; it indicates morning the way a clock hand indicates 3:14, it does not make it. So time is a sign a rhythm-keeper bears, referenced by naming the bearer, which is rule 1 (name the actor). The familiar `at <time>` is the English collapse that hides the bearer and treats the event as a place; allow it as shorthand, but it desugars to `when <rhythm-keeper> shows <time>`. Who shows 4:05: the clock today, the luminaries in Genesis, the reality's rhythm-keeper in pure act/fact. (The `at` of position, rule 18, is told apart by its object, a place versus a clock-reading.) The sync is the shared bearer: all who read the same rhythm-keeper's 4:05 agree on "now," which is why a wall-clock feels external, it is one being everyone reads. A clockless reality cannot say it at all; it has only order and named units.

14. **A prohibition subtracts, and where it meets a grant it wins.** A capability line grants (`a member can back a proposal`); a prohibition line subtracts (`no one can back it`, `no being but the founder can dissolve it`). A prohibition is a standing watch like any conditional (rule 6): while a proposal is embargoed the subtraction holds, and it falls away the moment the world stops matching. Where a grant and a prohibition both reach the same act, the prohibition wins, because a world's ables are built by adding capabilities and a prohibition is the only thing that can take one back; a forbidding a grant could override would forbid nothing. (That precedence is the one load-bearing claim, and it is an auth-semantics decision flagged for Tabor.)

15. **The bridge names two places at once, and it is asymmetric.** A cross-world act names two places joined by `::`, read `here :: there`. The left is always where you stand, full and yours, a place you may end `@me` (rule 11). The right is where the act lands, and it may be only a place with no being named, because you reach the position, not a particular presence there. One bridge, two stances; the act crosses it without you leaving the left, and by rule 7 the fact still resolves to the acting Name even as it lands on the far reality. (The `::` mark is the one new token this adds, flagged for your nod.)

16. **An ending seals a subject; rule 13 closes a span.** An ending is a one-way seal on a subject's capacity to bear new facts. After a being is done (`be:death`), a matter is done (its tombstone), or a Name is banished, that subject can never be the subject of a new act, yet every fact it already bears still stands (rule 7). This is distinct from rule 13: a closure act (`this was the evening set`) names a bounded span of time and turns it into a handle while the world keeps acting; an ending stops one subject from acting and is not a unit of time. The surface says `is done` or `is banished` and names what no longer happens, never the host seal (rule 3). A release (`be:release`, a hold let go) is reversible and is not an ending.

17. **An act may name a receiver, the being it hands something to.** An act may name a second being it hands something to, the receiver: `I give the drum to Claude`. The actor is still the acting Name (`I`, rule 9); the receiver is a named being the thing moves toward, not a fourth noun beside being, space, and matter. Possession and place follow the receiver by the act itself, so `I own` becomes `Claude owns` and the thing settles where the receiver can keep it, both turning on the real relations of rule 2, never a flavor verb for the move. The receiver is a property of the DO act, not a new construct: only a hand-over needs one, so no act other than a transfer carries it.

18. **A being is in a space, and it is the only mover of itself.** Position is a being's own relation to where it stands, said plainly: a being is `in` a space, or `at` a spot inside it, and is in one space at a time. This is a third real relation alongside `contains` and `owns` (rule 2), a being's, not a space's or a Name's. A being moves itself with a walk, never a possession and never a BE op: it `walks to` a spot, or `walks through` a doorway into another space, and crossing into a foreign space (the bridge of rule 15, named by its `<reality>#<history>/<space>` address) is the same walk with a further destination. The walk is the record (rule 3); you never write that it left a mark. A being is the one and only mover of itself.

19. **A kind is referenced consistently, never as a bare capital; `it` is the back-reference.** Once a kind is introduced (`A model is a matter type`), keep referring to it the same way: repeat the articled form (`a model accepts binary content`) or use the pronoun `it` / `It` (`It accepts binary content`). Never switch to a bare capitalized `Model`, that is a specific being's name by rules 5 and 10, and a kind is not a being. The pronoun `it` resolves to the kind just introduced (the block's subject); it is the first back-reference pronoun The Word approves, earned because a real program (`matter.word`) needs to describe a type over several clauses without the repetition or the capital confusion. (The same `it` will serve any subject a block is describing, not only a kind.) The general discipline this protects: the only intentional capitals in The Word are specific beings (`Cherub`, `Tabor`) and the Name-references `I` / `I`; sentence-initial capitals are ordinary English; everything else, kinds, ables, properties, ops, mid-sentence pronouns, stays lowercase. So a capital word that is not at a sentence start is a being, by construction, which is why a kind must never be written `Model`.

20. **A name is a single token; compound names use a hyphen.** A space, matter, being, or kind name is one token with no spaces, matching the substrate's own rules (verified: `SPACE_NAME_RE` = `/^[A-Za-z0-9][A-Za-z0-9._-]*$/`, `BEING_NAME_RE` = `/^[a-zA-Z0-9_-]{1,32}$/`, a matter type = `/^[a-z][a-z0-9-]*$/`). So write `a music-room`, not `a music room`; `model`, not two words. Hyphens and underscores are allowed (dots too, for spaces); a space is not. The name you write is the name stored, no hidden canonicalization. (This corrects the earlier shape-experiment drafts that wrote `music room`; they read as `music-room`. A free-text label, if a reality wants one, is a separate quality, not the referenceable name.)
