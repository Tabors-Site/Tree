# 26 — The one-token model IS the transformer (autoregression = the loop, KV cache = the fold) (summary + insight)

**TL;DR.** The "one Word at a time" model is not an *analogy* to a decoder-only LLM — at the generation level it **is** one. Autoregressive decode = read-the-face → fire-one-do → stamp → refold, line for line. **The KV cache is the fold**: append-only, each token's K/V computed once and sealed, the **causal mask = the parent-link** (every position sees only earlier ones). The transformer implements the invariants — append-only, past fixed, causality-not-clock — in silicon; we're rediscovering it, not fighting it.

**The efficiency knife (the whole answer to "isn't re-reading the face every token wasteful?").** The *misseeing* trap: keep the fold as our own thing, re-serialize it to a fresh text blob every moment, re-prompt from scratch — that throws the cache away and re-runs the full forward pass over the whole context every token (O(n²)). The *right* version: **let the KV cache BE the fold** — the being holds a cache, each act appends, the refold is the incremental forward pass the model already does for free.

**The clean split.** Rasterizing the face from the reels (vantage · which facts · supersession · ordering) is **context assembly, OUTSIDE the model**; firing the one do is the **native decode step, INSIDE**. Map: **fold ↔ KV cache · face-assembly ↔ prompt/cache construction (the retrieval logic) · the do ↔ the token step.** Don't make the transformer's internals match the multi-reel fold — that's the misseeing.

**The honest mismatch (and the real cost knob).** The cache is a single linear append-only line — it can't cheaply retract or reorder. So *change = append a newer fact the fold prefers* is **free within a turn** (later token, model leans recent), but **re-rasterizing across turns** — dropping stale facts, reordering by vantage — physically rebuilds the context and **evicts the cache**. That's where the cost sits: **append-and-roll vs re-rasterize-from-scratch.**

**The tie (it folds in the last three turns).** A decoder-only being is the **masked, causal right side**, generating word by word. The **encoder + the unmasked cross-attention box** is the slot where an external input attends in — which is exactly the **axis-two SENSE** (GenerativeMode.md). **Decoder = the Word-being acting (always Word, the pen). Encoder = a real sense entering through the membrane (any medium, the eyes).** The two axes are the two halves of the transformer.

## Insight — what this locks for the build
- **Live rasterization (25.md Pillar D) IS the context-assembly half.** Streaming the face fact-by-fact isn't a viewer gimmick — it is literally the prompt/KV-cache construction the LLM path needs. Build it to expose **both modes**: incremental **append-and-roll** (the common, cheap path — the model's free incremental pass; each per-fact stream event = one cache append) and full **re-rasterize** (when vantage/supersession changes the face — the cache-eviction cost knob).
- **All three cognitions consume the ONE face-stream** (Tabor): **portal** = the human watches the frame load / the generative face; **llm** = the stream *is* its KV-cache/prompt construction (append a fact = append to the cache); **scripted** = a being that **reacts per-fact** — *live reactive script beings*: a scripted cognition becomes a reactive function over the fact-stream, firing its one do the instant its condition lands, instead of waking only at moment boundaries. One face-stream, three consumers — the substrate-uniform-across-cognition doctrine realized as a **live reactive channel**.
- **Senses = the encoder slot, not the decode loop.** A real sense (the camera/effectful read) attends in through cross-attention; the being acts out through the decoder. The transformer already has the two-slot shape: **decode = act (Word), cross-attend = sense (any medium).** Keep them separate exactly as the diagram does.
- **This is the efficient floor for the whole one-token-being design** — and the reason not to invent our own re-prompt loop. The autoregression + KV cache already carry the chain natively; our job is the face-assembly (retrieval) outside, and the one-do (decode) inside.
