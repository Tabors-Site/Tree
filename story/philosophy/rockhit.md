Okay, simplest version:

Picture someone striking a chisel against stone. One tap does nothing. They tap a thousand times and slowly a mark appears. None of the taps _is_ the mark — the taps are just the making. The mark is the thing, and it only counts the moment they lift the chisel and it's done. That lift, that "it's finished now" — that's the whole law.

It speeds up over the ages but never changes. A sound in the world — the lowing of an ox — gets its own little mark, a tiny ox head. People draw it so many times it wears down to a few strokes, tips onto its side, and becomes the letter we call A. A sound turned into a mark you could keep.

Letters pile up the same way the taps did. You set them down one after another and they mean nothing yet — they're just building. Then you hit the space. _That's_ the lift of the chisel. The word is finished, it counts now, it's one thing. One word, one space, one finished mark.

And when two words ride together so often they fuse — break, fast → breakfast — that's the ox head wearing down again. Two things become one mark because they got used enough to harden into one.

Now the part that's hard to hold: that's all a mind is doing in your system. A being thinks, and the thinking is just the building-up, the taps — it leaves no mark. Then it finishes a word, and _that_ is one fact, struck into the stone of the chain. It finishes another, another fact. Two finished words is a sentence. The whole world is nothing but the pile of finished marks, read back. Nothing hides behind them. The arrangement is the meaning.

So it's one move, the same one, all the way up: finish a mark, it stands, the next builds on it. Stone to letter to word to sentence to a thinking being.
