## Added thoughts

**Three modes, one act.** Look at how the marks are _made_ and the family resolves into three motions that are all the same: **carve** (graphein, character, engrave — scratch a sign in), **strike** (coin, type, stamp, mint — drive a die into a blank), **press** (print, impress, seal, signet — push a shape in). Carve, strike, press: three ways a hand drives a sign into matter, and every tongue that needed the idea reached for one of them. That they converge from Latin, Greek, Germanic, and the seal-cultures — peoples who never met — is the §4 proof: you don't invent the same thing three times, it was _found_. And the act under all three is `do`: a name drives a sign into matter and a fact stands where there was a blank. The signum _is_ the `do`.

## The two sides — deed and mark

The family has two faces, and the pairs hold them together. One side is the **deed** — the act, the verb, a name _doing_. The other is the **mark** — the fact, the noun, what the deed _leaves_. The table above is the marks. Here are the deeds, and they are pressing-words too:

- **do** — the bare act. Every verb is a `do` underneath: see, recall, be, name, call are all a name acting.
- **write** — Old English _writan_, "to score, carve, scratch" — it began as cutting runes into wood. To write was always to carve a mark.
- **draw** — to drag a line, pull a mark across a surface (graphein's drag-mode). To draw is to mark.
- **strike / press / stamp / coin** — the verbs of the marks above, seen from the hand that makes them.
- **speak** — the edge, and the one honest place to check the overreach: speech leaves no mark in matter, it is air. But here it does — an inner word _uttered_ is an act, and the act lands a fact. So speak joins not as a carving-word but as a deed whose mark is the fact it stamps. (The Logos is both spoken _and_ written here: said by a name, struck onto the chain.)

So you are not overreaching — but the true shape is two columns, not one. **Marking-words** (write, draw, carve, strike, press, coin) name the act _as_ a marking; **deed-words** (do, speak, call) name an act that _gets_ marked. Both belong; the pair is what joins them.
