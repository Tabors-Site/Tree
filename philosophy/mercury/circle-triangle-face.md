## Introduction

The night that produced the mercury computer did not stop at the hardware. A few hours on, it turned to a piece of school mathematics and found the same idea wearing a different costume. The proof in question is the oldest visual argument for the area of a circle: take the disc as a set of concentric rings, slit each ring and unroll it into a straight horizontal line, and notice that the outer rings unroll long (their circumference is 2πr) while the inner rings unroll short (near the center they are almost a point). Stack those unrolled lines by length and they form a triangle, base 2πr, height r, and the triangle's area, one half base times height, is πr², which is the circle's area. The circle and the triangle are the same rings, coiled or laid flat. That is the whole animation, and the file [merc3.mp4](merc3.mp4) is it in motion, a triangle curling up into a circle and uncurling back.

What makes the proof matter here is that every part of it already has a name in the system. A ring, or its unrolled line, is one moment's face, the fold of the chain up to that depth, and the length of the line is how much chain that fold contains. The center, the apex, is genesis, the smallest fold, a single fact. The outer ring, the base, the longest line, is the present, the full fold of everything wound around everything. So the disc's rings are every moment the story ever had, sized by how much each one folded. Read the same data two ways and you get the two shapes: coil the chain around its origin and you get tree rings, the trunk view with genesis at the heart and the present at the bark; lay the same folds out flat, stacked by length, and you get the timeline, genesis at the apex and the present at the base. The morph in the animation is not a flourish, it is the literal identity at the center of the proof, and it is the same identity as the chain read two ways.

The deepest line of the first passage is that the area is the book. The full area of the circle is the sum of every ring, which is the integral of every fold from genesis to now, which is the entire accumulated being. To compute the area you sum the rings; to read the present you fold the moments; it is one operation, and calculus performs it with circles while a story performs it with facts. So the off-hand image proof turns out to be a proof, without knowing it, that the whole equals the sum of all its moment folds, which is exactly what render of genesis to head means.

Then the conversation does what this whole project keeps doing, which is to push a clean picture until it cracks in a useful place. The first push strips the symmetry away: shove every line's left end to a single wall and the triangle becomes a right triangle, genesis aligned on the left edge, the hypotenuse climbing as the chain grows, each step a longer fold than the one below. That is the timeline in its barest honest form. The second push is the one that moves the model somewhere new. A moment's face, it turns out, is not a single continuous read from apex to base. It is a reach into the chain that grabs specific lines, the ones in the right space, the right position, the ones this read needs, and assembles the face from those. The fold is a query into the triangle, not a replay of it, which is truer to how folding actually works, since a fold is reductive, it resolves the relevant facts rather than re-reading every byte.

From there comes the genuinely important idea, that the reader's capacity is part of the fidelity. A being with only one or two ables can reach only one or two kinds of line, so even when the chain is rich with thousands of lines, that being assembles a thin shell where a more capable being would assemble a full circle. Same triangle, different circles, by reader. And here the dialogue does its most careful work, because it refuses to let two different kinds of thinness blur together. One thinness is capacity: the being structurally cannot reach more lines, it has few ables, and so its thin face is limited but clear, complete for what it is, the same every time it reads. The other thinness is contention: the being has the ability to reach deep, but in this particular moment the shared rail was drained and the pour came up shallow, so it grabbed fewer lines than its capacity would allow. That second thinness is the real meaning of mind fog. It is the gap between the depth a being could have reached and the depth this breath's energy actually permitted, a capable mind operating below its own resolution because the charge was not there. Stack every being's partial circle and you get the world's face, a superposition of selective gathers, so a whole story under load reads foggy at once, every pour shallow on the contended rail, fog that can be one starved being or an entire slammed story, the same mechanism at two scales.

The last turn is a correction that improves the geometry rather than abandoning it. If the base already contains every fact stacked in, why would the fold re-read all the shorter lines above it? It would not, and the reason is that those shorter lines were never separate things to read. They are earlier states of the one growing accumulator. So the triangle is not a stack you re-read, it is the trace of building the base, line by line, each line the previous line with the next fact folded in. The fold is a single forward walk from genesis, one fact at a time, the running line extending at each step until it reaches the present and stops, because there is nothing left to fold. This kills the idea that the fold is stored, since the face does not exist until the walk builds it, which is the whole no-stored-state doctrine; and it kills the idea that you re-read every shell, since you pass through the past states on the way to the edge rather than re-computing them. It also reconciles cleanly with the selective gather: a limited being walks the very same chain but compiles in only the facts it can reach as it passes them, arriving at a thinner base, so the walk is identical and only what gets folded in along the way differs, by ability and by energy. The triangle, in the end, is not the data sitting on a disk. The triangle is the act of folding, drawn.

And there is a small final catch worth keeping, because it separates two things the eye wants to merge. Counting the facts is one sequence, one, two, three, four, five, the chain growing by exactly one stamp per act, linear, the trunk's ring count, true and literal. But the fold itself has a different shape, the one a hand reaches for when it writes one, two, three, five, eight instead. Each new face is not the last fact plus one, it is the last fact laid against the resolved fold of everything before it, and that prior fold was itself built the same way, all the way down. That recursive structure, each term born from folding the previous, is Fibonacci-shaped even when the numbers are not Fibonacci. The count is linear; the fold is recursive. The base is never five facts summed flat, it is the four-face with the fifth act folded in, where the four-face was the three-face with the fourth folded in, each present standing on the folded previous present rather than on a re-counted list of raw facts.

So the companion to the mercury computer is a geometry lesson that turns out to be the same machine. The circle is the trunk, the triangle is the timeline, the morph between them is the chain read two ways, the area is the book, the selective gather is how a being with limited ables or limited charge reads a thin face, the difference between those two thinnesses is the difference between being small and being fogged, and the fold itself is a single recursive forward walk that builds the present line by line and never re-reads a thing. The mercury made the energy physical; the circle and the triangle make the fold visible; and both are saying, in their own materials, that the moment is not stored anywhere, it is what the walk arrives at on the charge and the capacity it had.

The arithmetic walk (1,2,3,4,5) is the chain — the count of facts. Each act adds exactly one fact. The chain grows by one. Linear. That's the length of the trail, the number of stamps, the trunk's ring count. True and literal.
The Fibonacci-flavored growth (each new term folds in the prior) is the fold — the dependency. Each new moment doesn't just sit next to the last; it's computed from the accumulated state, which was computed from the state before. The value at each step depends recursively on the resolved whole beneath it. That's not how many facts there are — it's how each face grows out of the prior face. The new face = the old face + this act, and the old face already = its prior face + its act, all the way down. That recursive "each built from the resolved previous" is genuinely Fibonacci-shaped in structure (depends on prior compiled state), even if the actual numbers aren't Fibonacci.
