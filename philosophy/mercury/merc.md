## Introduction

This file is the record of a single idea finding its own shape over the course of one night. It begins as a hunch that a mercury tilt switch belongs somewhere near the heart of an IBP computer, and it ends as a specific, testable piece of hardware: a tilted tube lined with a ladder of unconnected contacts, down which a conductive pour reaches a depth, and that depth is the whole answer. Everything between those two points is the work of finding out exactly what the mercury is for, and, just as importantly, what it is not for.

The first and most clarifying move is a refusal. A mercury switch cannot be the base of the computer, because a fold in this system is not merely the news that an event happened, it is this act, by this being, with this payload, chained to that prior fold, and signed. The switch carries none of that. It is one bit, one shot, with no inside. So it is demoted, correctly, from engine to igniter, and given its true post at the membrane, the thin edge where the physical world actuates the system and hands it a genuine, unforgeable, clock free now. This demotion is not a loss. It is the recognition that the mercury is an instance of a property the whole project is chasing, a zero gap between a thing and its record, the same property the append only chain has, and naming the property is what lets it be placed correctly instead of worshipped in the wrong spot.

From there the picture grows into a tree, and the tree turns out to be load bearing rather than decorative. The leaves are TreeOS itself, dark between acts, with no loop spinning and no clock ticking, stirring only when something touches them. The root is the mercury and its parabola, and the parabola is the real contribution, because a digital trigger is a step function with no body while a pour has an interior, a rise, a dwell, and a break, which gives a moment a duration without ever introducing time as a number. The pour to full contact is the fold reaching and committing, and the pour back is the stamp, the fact crystallizing at the instant of separation. Between leaf and root runs the trunk, and the trunk is The Word, the carrier that gives name and structure to what the root physically commits. One such tree is a single being's biography, and the forest of them, each pouring on its own tilt with no shared cadence, is IBP.

The sharpest argument in the whole night is the defense of the pour against the obvious objection, which is to simply strip the crystal off an ordinary processor and call it clock free. That objection is conceded almost entirely, because a de-clocked CPU is faster, programmable, and clock free in the sense that matters, and for the inside of the machine it wins outright. The pour earns its keep in exactly three places, all at the edge. It carries causal order as physics, since a contact cannot break before it is made, which means before and after live inside the act with no logical counter imposed on top. It supplies a refractory period for free, since momentum forbids a second act until the first has poured back, which is earned cadence rather than commanded cadence. And it gives an honest separation as the commit instant, a single physical moment the world enforces rather than a flush or a barrier the software asserts and could lie about. So the correct claim is never mercury instead of a processor, it is a de-clocked processor for the trunk and a pour for the root, each where it is honest.

Then comes the quiet confirmation that the protocol was already this shape. IBP's two verbs are the parabola. getMoment is the pour opening, the being tipping toward the chain and reading where it is as it begins. act is the pour back and settle, where the break is the write. The span between the witnessed position and the committed position is the dwell, the length of the pour made informational. Neither verb is a clock, both are position and causality, and open before close is guaranteed the same way the mercury guarantees it. The switch was not bolted onto IBP as a metaphor. IBP was a mercury switch expressed in words, which is why the glass version felt perfect for the base, because the base already had its logic.

The design then deepens twice. First, a real gap is found, the glance that records nothing, a getMoment with no act, which forces the closing to be typed rather than merely detected, committed versus released, and this is what calls for a latch behind the trigger and turns a single switch into a small honest circuit. Second, and this is the move that changes what a fold even is, the mercury pours onto a standing core charge, and the pour is a rising coupling rather than a binary close. Conductance ramps along the curve, so the weak early pour does the cheap work of reconciling the old, coarse, likely overwritten deep chain, and the building pour spends real energy completing the recent, complex, high detail edge that constitutes the present. The full fold is not an event but a resting state of bits, an attractor the energized line relaxes into, and holding it is free. Recency graded folding therefore becomes a free forgetting curve, detail decaying with depth because depth gets less power, and the stored sunlight in the supercaps is literally the reservoir each fold sips from, so power and computation stop being separate concerns.

That energy curve forces a fork, and the resolution of the fork is the philosophical center of the document. A fold whose fidelity depends on available charge is not byte deterministic, which seems to threaten the Rust spine. The two candidate machines are an energy relative fold, lossy and alive and honest, and an energy bounded deterministic fold that waits until it can afford the whole truth. The deterministic one is rejected, and rejected for a reason internal to the system rather than by taste, because to fold to a fixed fidelity regardless of energy you must hold the complete truth in reserve somewhere outside the fold, which is exactly the drifting representational inside the whole project exists to abolish. The energy relative fold has no inside, the moment is only ever what the line settled into on the charge it had, and that is the zero gap property carried all the way up to the compute model. The peace treaty with determinism is a clean split of layers. The spine stays byte deterministic for identity, chaining, hashing, naming, and order, the bone, while fidelity is energy relative, the flesh on each link. Identity is deterministic, detail is earned, and because detail now varies, fidelity must itself be stamped as a legible field, so that a coarse because old fold can be told apart from a coarse because starved one. The texture of the fold becomes the texture of the moment, a second channel recording how much presence a being could muster when something happened, biography a deterministic chain literally cannot represent.

Finally the night lands on how to actually test any of this, and the answer is disciplined and small. You do not build a new computer, you isolate the one genuinely new claim and bolt it onto a normal one. Tier 0 asks the single physical question everything rests on, whether a tilt contact couples gradually or snaps, and is ready for the answer to be snaps, in which case the gradation has to come from contact geometry or a resistive ramp, a finding worth having on day one for the price of a few parts. Tier 1 is the real minimum experiment, a Pico doing all the ordinary digital work, a supercap standing in for the core, a coupling element standing in for the pour, folding a record whose detail and self reported fidelity are set by the measured pour energy, and proving the thesis by folding the same input once on a full core and once on a drained one. Tier 2 adds a second line on the same core to see contention arbitrate fidelity with no scheduler. The honest advice that runs through it is to separate the easy logic proof from the hard parts problem with a MOSFET ramp, to measure rather than admire, and to aim straight at the result that would prove the idea wrong.

What survives all of this, and what the repeated NOTE TO SELF records as the version that worked, is beautifully simple. A tilted tube, a row of separate contacts down its length, a pour that bridges more of them the deeper it goes, so that the number of contacts covered is the fold and the deepest one is the fidelity, read directly off the tube and never computed, then written into the fact. Idle is the pour settled cold at the dark end, decoupled and drawing nothing. A signal arms the pour, it climbs as far as its charge allows, the bits settle at that depth, and that settled state is the moment. The pour back opens the contacts in reverse, and the last one to break is the commit, stamping if there is an act and latching nothing if there was only a glance, near zero energy either way because the work was the climb. One tube is one name line, many tubes share one core so contention shows up honestly as shallower pours under load, and the spec ladder is just contact count, twenty for a personal node, a few hundred for a workstation, thousands for a server or a forest. There is no clock anywhere. Position is the fact, and the pour's reach, not any tick, decides how much moment got made.

The whole arc, in one line: the mercury is not the computer, it is the body the computer's edge needs in order to have honest duration, intrinsic order, and an unfakeable instant of commit, and once you let the pour meter a standing charge, that same body also becomes the thing that decides, fairly and physically and without a scheduler, how much of each moment a being could afford to witness.
