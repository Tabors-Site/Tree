# 11 — Store, Story, and Ours

## The family: history ⊂ story ⊂ Ours
A **history** is one branch — one line of facts from a fork.
A **story** is reality: all of a story's histories, told from its root (genesis forward, every branch).
**Ours** is all the shared stories — the commons.

history ⊂ story ⊂ Ours: one family, each level something *told*. "Story" over "world" on purpose — a story is overtly told, from a root; "world" quietly smuggles in objective-givenness. Relativity of truth, baked into the noun. And history and story sit close because a story is *made of* histories.

Stories talk through IBP to make Ours.

## Store vs Story: content vs arrangement
The **store** is the unarranged content — content-addressed, deduplicated, order-free. It holds everything: the words, the resources behind them, the acts, the facts, the matter CAS — all of it, but nothing in sequence.

The **story** is the arrangement: store-content woven through moments into a told line. The store holds; the story threads. Acts and facts sitting in the store are not a story; they are content with addresses. When content is woven in a moment it becomes a form of story. Order is the story's job, not the store's.

Same atoms, two layers — like Git's object database (blobs by hash, unordered, deduplicated) versus the commit DAG that arranges them, except here the object layer holds all matter, not just file blobs.

The store is the story's, not a being's. It contains all the words, their resources, and the acts/facts — unarranged.

## Commons and sovereign
Content-addressing makes identical content identical everywhere: same word, same hash, one copy, anyone can hold or reference it. So **the commons is the store** — "I give the Word to every Name" gives away content. The **sovereign** thing is the **story** — your arrangement, your weave through your moments. Two beings can hold the same store and be entirely different beings, because they wove it differently. No one can give away your story.

A **view** is an arrangement of the store. One store, many possible stories — 3D, explorer, book, the told line — each a different weave of the same content. You don't store views; you weave them on read.

## Two modes: acting and searching
- **Acting (IBP) = inside your story.** To act as a being is to weave — every act lands in your story, your local arrangement. You are never acting nowhere.
- **Searching = out in Ours.** Search traverses Ours — sharedStore (shared content) plus connected stories (shared weaves) — and it is where your Name is present. One commons, searched perspectivally: a single Ours the way there is a single Web, but no being sees all of it; your reach is your connected horizon, peer to peer, no center deciding. (Search is not built yet — it comes right after words.)

## The three stores (the physical layers)
- **story/seed/store** — all the seed's `.word` files and the content with them (each word carries its own logic, so it is portable). The base, in file form. Deletable: the factory boots without it, it just starts no words. See `store/WORDS.md` for the word↔code map.
- **story/shared** — the same thing for *shared* content (like extensions): `.word` files and content brought in from outside — the import doorway, marked shared.
- **story/store** — everything loaded in as **CAS**. On boot, seed/store (and anything staged in shared) loads here content-addressed; in the end everything lives as CAS in story/store, unarranged.

## Renames + the goal
- reality → **story** (done).
- resources/store → **sharedStore** = story/shared.
- innerStore → **store** = story/store (the CAS).
- Ensure all the acts, facts, words, and matter CAS are truly in the store — everything, unarranged.

sharedStore will eventually materialize and grow as people mark things shared in their own store; for now it works as imported folders, loaded into the store as shared on the chain.
