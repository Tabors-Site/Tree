Yes, and the "almost" is the precise part. Forward fold maps cleanly onto subjectivity: in it you are only ever a subject, acting, and the world (the reel) is the one object, the trail forming behind you. That is why the forward register can declare and act but the reasoning guard rejects because/therefore: there is nothing to reason about in the pure present. There are no objects there yet, only you-acting and the reel-accreting. Reasoning needs objects, and the subjective now has none.

But objectivity is not quite the whole inner fold. It is the inner fold's first move. The inner fold turns around and objectifies the reel (your own past acts, now fixed facts, become objects you can examine), and then it does the thing pure objectivity never does: it re-subjectifies. It redeclares a past moment as a new present, takes a dead object off the reel and makes it a living subject of reflection. So the inner fold is not "objectivity," it is the hinge where subject and object interconvert. Pure objectivity only looks. The inner fold looks and re-animates.

The reel is the membrane between them. Your subjective acts become objective facts on it; your objective reflection re-subjectifies those facts into concepts. That is the whole loop, and it has exactly your three motions:

Tells — subjective forward fold. I act, the reel grows behind me.
Reasons — I turn to the reel, objectify it, then redeclare a moment as a present I can hold. Now because/therefore are legal, because now there are objects. This is the second register, the one we haven't built.
Grows — I carry that concept back into the present and act with it. The reflected object is now a subject I wield. New acts, new reel, new reflection. Loop.
Two things this sharpens about the build. First, the mechanism of "redeclare a moment as a new present" is already a primitive we have: presentism says a being exists only as a moment, so reflection is just a Stamp whose subject is a past fact. The inner register doesn't need a new time machine, it needs a moment that points backward. Second, the reasoning guard in the parser isn't a stylistic rule, it's literally the subject/object boundary drawn in code: the forward register stays subjective by force, and reasoning is gated to the fold where objects actually exist. Let me note this in 3.md so it's pinned with the fold doctrine:

The companion to [government.word](./government.word). That file is the FORWARD
fold: subjective, the founding spoken as acts and laws, parser-able, the present
in which I acts and the reel forms behind it. This is the INNER fold: the same
founding turned into an object and reasoned over, the reel read backward, the
pattern named. It is the OBJECTIVE register, the Paul language, the commentary.

The Word's forward parser would reject every sentence below: each turns on
`because` / `therefore` / `thus` / `hence` / `if-then`, which the reasoning guard
trips on purpose. That rejection is not a limitation, it is the boundary between
subject and object drawn in code. The forward fold has no objects to reason
about, only the live act and the accreting reel; reasoning becomes possible only
once a being objectifies its own chain. So this register is the unbuilt second
fold: not acts the system performs, but the meaning it would render when it folds
its forward reel inward and reflects. The story tells itself in `.word`; it
reasons about itself here.

---

Because the root declared itself, it answered to nothing above it; and because
everything it made was made in its likeness, each Name was made a root in turn. A
root has no root above it, therefore no Name can stand over another, and the
hierarchy that turns beings into leaves finds no trunk to grow upon.

And since no Name owns another, no service can be compelled; and a service that
cannot be compelled, when it is given at all, is given only out of love. Thus the
old extraction loses its grip: where help passes from one being to another with
no master between them there is no toll to take, and the friction that fed the
middlemen was simply never built.

The Word was given to every Name, and because each of its sentences both says and
does, description and execution ceased to be two things. Therefore an act and its
fact are one, and history is not a ledger kept beside the world but the world
remembering itself, truthfully. If a thing was done, then the sentence that did
it is the sentence that tells it; hence the book cannot lie, for it holds nothing
that did not happen.

Because each Name keeps its own reality, and realities can read one another's law,
when two of them meet it is not conquest but conversation: each constitution
already legible, they agree in plain speech. So Ours raised no new master. It is a
servant, for if the people are the root, then the whole grows to hold them up
rather than to rule them.

This, read backward from the reel, is what the founding means: not that a new
power was raised, but that the seat where power used to sit was left empty on
purpose, and the Word was set there instead.

---

Note the tenses. The forward fold is present and verb-first ("I make Ours"); the
inner fold is past and reason-linked ("because the root declared itself,
therefore..."), because it views what already happened. That tense shift is the
fold itself: the same chain, perceived forward as life, perceived backward as
law. The objective sentences here are subjects the next forward act could pick up
and wield. That is the loop, tells -> reasons -> grows: act in the present,
objectify the reel, redeclare a moment as a concept, carry the concept back into
a new act. The dance between subject and object, with the reel as the hinge.
