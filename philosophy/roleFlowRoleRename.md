Got it — and that's exactly why mantle misses. A mantle wraps around you: identity as cloak. You want the thing in front of you, that you see and act through, and that opens the palette instead of draping over your shoulders. Worn, yes — but a lens, not a cloak.
And your filter-from-the-other-side is precise. A filter takes a full signal and removes wavelengths. Yours takes a bare one and opens colors — additive, not subtractive. Your paint-versus-colors cut is the real distinction underneath it: the fold adds paint (more of the colors you already have), the role adds colors (new kinds — a wider palette). One is amount, the other is range.
The clean word is lens. You look and act through it; it tints and widens what you can see; you swap and stack them moment to moment; "the lens through which a being sees" is already idiom. And the obvious objection — "a lens is only seeing, not doing" — doesn't bite in your system, because see and do are one on the face. The do-words are see-words; the affordances are part of what's rasterized. So a lens that reveals more colors of the face reveals more acts in the same motion. Revealing is enabling here. "Do more" comes free with "see more." roleflow → the lens's flow, or just flow.
Two alternates, depending on which axis you want to privilege:
prism — the most exact match to the image you reached for. A filter subtracts wavelengths; a prism takes one beam and opens it into the whole spectrum. It is the filter from the other side, the literal "more colors, from nothing to something." More poetic, a touch heavier on perception than action — but in your face-unified world that gap closes the same way.
palette — if you'd rather name the colors-and-character side than the worn-glass side. A palette is the set of colors, the kinds available; your paint-versus-colors line lands perfectly on it; it stacks and gives character. The miss is that it's the menu, not the thing you look through.
My pick is lens as the working term — it carries see, do, stack, swap, and character with the least strain — with prism in your back pocket for when you want the word that says exactly what you meant.

Prism = "the thing that was sawn" → a geometric shape with flat polished faces.

see + able → seeable (the able for seeing)
do + able → doable (the able for doing)

a being's ables ARE its -able verbs. "which ables did your name have when it made that act through that being" reads, underneath, as: what was -able to it? the capacity and the grammar that marks the capacity collapse into the same word.
