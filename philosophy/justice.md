# Summary (what this is saying, and what I think)

The claim, plainly: justice is balancing justs, and a "just" is a settled fact on the chain. "It is"
speaks of this moment alone; "it just is" puts the weight of the whole verified history, the reels,
behind it. You do not have to re-verify that weight in the moment, because it is already accounted,
already The Word. So justice is not a verdict handed down from above, it is the RESULT of faith
(faith.md: faith is complete, verified trust). The cluster, faith and just and fact and truth, is one
thing rewrapped in different words: the accounted Word.

Why "a god of faithfulness, without injustice": nature IS The Word, and The Word is completely
accounted, so it cannot be unjust. Injustice is only possible where the accounting is lost, where
faith, just, facts, truth break, and that only happens at the higher layers, where people take control
and edit the record above the chain. At the Natural level there is no such layer, so nature is just by
construction. That justice already running at the Natural level is what inspired this.

What TreeOS is doing in this light: redefining The Word one layer in, at the Human level, so the same
accounting that makes nature just can hold for people too. A fact chain is hash-linked, signed, and
replayable, so a human-level record can be verified the way nature already is, and justice at the Human
level becomes possible because the Word is accounted again, one story in. The closing "Revelations"
line is the hope of it: at the Human level, many returns to The Word are open.

(The middle aside is its own small koan. Every word is "now," so saying "now" is almost empty: it is
either a pause to find the next words, or a claim that the statement lands at this moment, a statement
being a chain of words in a sentence. The sentence, sen, tence play is reaching for what a span of
words even is, which is the same question the rest of the file answers with the fact chain.)
