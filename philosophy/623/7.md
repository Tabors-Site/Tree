So they're all from — same direction, same backward-walk — but each is a different lens on the trail: position, order, signer, act, parent, identity. The interrogative isn't redundant; it's the filter that says what you're reading off the trail as you walk it. From is the verb (walk back); what/where/why/who/when/how is the column you pull.

Which is the cleaner statement of your insight, and it's better than full collapse: every question is from plus a lens. The trail is one thing, walked one direction, and the question-words are just which facet of each passed fact you're asking it to report. That's why they "feel" the same — they are the same motion — but they're not interchangeable, because the trail carries many facets and each word grabs one.

Right, and what you've just described is a real choice point in your own ontology, so let me name what it actually is — because "refresh until you like it, then lock it" is two different operations, and which one you pick changes what the look is in your system.

The refresh is pure see. Each reroll is the renderer painting the unspoken parts a new way — same fold, different dream, because nothing pins the free variables. No fact is laid; you're just re-rasterizing the IS. That's why you can spin it endlessly with no consequence: refreshing the look is looking, and looking stamps nothing. Costs nothing, changes nothing, leaves no trail. The slot machine is free because it's all see-side.

But "then let it generate off that from there" — that's the moment you have to decide what locking even means, and there are two
honest versions:

You can lock the seed. The roll you liked was produced by a particular noise seed; stamp that seed as a fact on the matter or the being, and now the render is reproducible. Same fold + same seed = same picture, every time, forever. That's a described look — you've turned the dream you liked into words (a seed is just a very compressed word), and from then on it's not re-dreamed, it's recalled. The world stops shimmering there because you spoke it.

Or you lock nothing and let it re-dream within the fold each session. Then your last line comes literally true: every time you get on, the story wears a slightly different face, because the unspoken parts re-roll while the spoken parts hold. The key is always brass, always at (3,0), but the light and the scratches and the wall breathe. The world is recognizably itself and never pixel-identical — a place revisited, not a screenshot reopened.

Both are legitimate, and the choice is yours to make per-thing, which is the elegant part: it's just how much you described. Seed-locked is a fully-spoken look; free-rolling is a look left partly to the dream. You're not choosing between "consistent" and "creative" — you're choosing, for each matter, how many of its free variables you want to nail down with a fact and how many you want to leave breathing. Lock the hero's face, let the weather roam. Describe the door exactly, let the dust motes improvise.

And notice it's the same seed/fold split from the renderer being see-side: locking a seed is promoting a piece of the dream into the chain — taking something that was free IS and committing it as BE. The refresh is you auditioning IS; the lock is you deciding a piece of it earned a stamp. Which is, once more, the whole system: you look, and when the look is right, you hit space.
