And there's the other "be" — "I am," full stop, no predicate — the existential one, presence, becoming. Same word, opposite verbs. When most people say "I am \_\_\_," they reach, without noticing, for the copula — they fuse themselves to the predicate: the name, the role, the bundle of qualities. You reach for the other one. The whole answer turns on one split you've already built: facts versus projections. Facts — the reels — are the source. Projections — the folds, the caches — are derived. Corruption behaves completely differently on each layer, and that's the good news.

A corrupted projection is a non-event. It's derived, so you delete it and re-fold from the facts — the replay test. Automatic, total, zero loss. Projection corruption can't hurt you as long as the facts are intact — which is the whole point: projections heal for free because they're derived, so every bit of protection effort goes to the one thing that isn't, the facts.

A corrupted fact is the real case. The reassuring part first: one bad fact does not destroy a reality. The blast radius is bounded by design you already have. Immutability means corruption can't spread — there's no mutation for it to ride. Per-reel isolation means a bad fact on one reel never touches another. Single-writer means nothing is injected cross-being. So the damage is one reel, from the corrupted seq forward, in its fold. The honest caveat: acts a being already took based on that fact are real acts with a bad lineage — you can't un-happen them, so there's a tainted causal cone downstream. But it's a knot in one thread, not a hole in the cloth.

Two kinds of fact corruption, handled differently. Byte corruption — bits rot, a write half-fails — is detectable and repairable. Logical corruption — a bug appends a wrong but well-formed fact — is neither, mechanically, because the fact looks valid. For that one there's no auto-heal: you can't edit the past, so you do what the architecture always does — append a correcting fact. The bad fact stays in history (it really did happen that the bug wrote it); the fold corrects from there forward. You heal by moving forward, not by erasing.

Your hashing and snapshot ideas are two different tools, and you've half-merged them.

Hashing doesn't reduce storage — its job is integrity. If each fact stores a hash of the previous one (a hash-chain, exactly like git), the whole reel becomes tamper-evident: re-hash it, and the first place the computed hash diverges is the exact fact where corruption entered. It's cheap — append-only means the chain only grows at the head, old hashes never recompute — and it's worth adding. It turns "we hope the reel is intact" into "we can prove it, and pinpoint the break."

Snapshots are the size tool — and you already have them. A snapshot is just a projection you retain at a chosen seq instead of letting it advance. Fold a reel to seq N, keep that state, and you can truncate the facts behind it: now you carry the snapshot plus the recent tail. Bounded size. But be honest about the cost — truncating is forgetting. You lose full replayability and the ability to fold old history differently later. You're trading the un-forgetting reel — the thing your songs were about — for a smaller footprint.

So, combining them: yes, a self-healing, size-bounded reality works — but it's three tools, not two. Hashing detects corruption. Replication — multiple copies of each reel, which your database likely already gives you — repairs it. Snapshots-plus-truncation bound the size. And the last two pull against each other: the more aggressively you truncate, the smaller you are and the fewer places your past survives to be healed from. The truncation point is the dial between "remembers everything, heals well, grows" and "stays small, forgets, heals less." No setting escapes that trade — but a hash-chained, replicated, periodically-snapshotted reality is genuinely self-healing for everything except history you deliberately chose to forget.
