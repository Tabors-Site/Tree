## Decisions locked

- Name: **The Word**. Extension: `.word`.
- Surface is **word-shaped** (natural-language sentences), not curly-brace syntax.
- Boundary: **world = everything an agent does** (I_AM, every Name, every being); **host = the engine that runs acts** (stamper, chain, fold, signing, transport, parser, evaluator). The WHAT is word; the HOW is host.
- `fact.action` stays (canon-frozen); an in-flight `act` becomes `action` only at the seal.
- **Grammar stays minimal.** Core nouns (being / space / matter) and verbs (see / do / summon / be / name) are approved. Extended grammar (adjectives, adverbs, pronouns, possessives, plurals, articles) is unapproved; add a construct only when a real program forces it (Tabor's flag, see the Status note in 1.md). Locked exception so far: articles as kind-versus-being marking (rule 10). Pending Tabor's nod: quantifiers (`most` / `every` / `no`) and plurals (forced by the governance commons); the `::` bridge token (rule 15) and the prohibition-outranks-grant precedence (rule 14), both forced by the new domain examples.
- Sequencing: shape experiment now; the conversion sweep waits until the Name / Being / Soul refactor lands.
- **Time has no kernel clock.** Native time is the chain's order plus named bounded units (closure acts: `this was the first day`), both clockless. Any rhythm, including human wall-time, is a being that beats, watched relationally (rules 12, 13). A clock being (the day-four apparatus) is optional, summoned by a reality that wants duration / human-labels / sync; its single escape hatch (`host: readClock`) is the only place the host clock touches the world. Remove it and order, units, folds, and federation survive. Genesis-grounded (light-as-quality on day one vs the rhythm apparatus on day four). Answers the parked pulse question.
- **The Word and facts.** The Word's canonical form is its AST (a typed object), not its prose; "word vs structured" is a false choice. A fact = the act's Word-AST (world, hashed/signed) + the host chain-envelope (hash / prev / sig / seq / branch). The parser runs at authoring (prose to AST); the AST is signed/federated/verified, so determinism rides the AST, not re-parsed prose. Display renderings are generated on demand (any language, no drift); a fact may keep the authored utterance as provenance (rule 7). Rendering the whole chain from the root in first person yields the reality as a creation narrative (`I am that I am. I create heaven...`), forced by rules 9 and 11: the graft@root rendered IS the reality as Word. Captured in [1.md](./1.md) ("The Word and the fact-chain").
- **Surface grammar, first eighteen rules** (found with Tabor, detailed in [4.md](./4.md)): (1) a summon's intent is active and names what the summoned being does; (2) possession uses the real relations `contains` / `owns`, not flavor verbs; (3) the surface never says "stamp" or "fold" (acts auto-record; those are host); (4) time is **relational** (reference concrete world events and other beings' acts, the harmony pattern); no time-system or reliable elapsed-time exists yet, so seconds and even abstract moment-counts are not expressible; (5) a capital word is a specific being (`Cherub`, `Tabor`), lowercase is a kind or a handle, and the True Name is never written in The Word (it lives in the act records, surfaces only as a reference: `I` / `Name` / `I_AM`, never its key value); (6) conditionals (`if` / `when` / `while`) are dormant standing watches, not predictions, no fact or moment until they fire, and the firing is the first thing on a chain; (7) grammatical person is perspective, not a new operation: "I commit" and "Tabor commits" are the same act resolved to the acting Name; every act is already a speech act, so self-declarations need no new fact type, just person-resolution (`I` / `me` / `self` to the acting Name); (8) a role is a noun you can `be`, the kind-of-a-being (a class; the being is the instance), declared `A X is a Y`, parallel to matter-kinds (`a thing is a drum`) and space-kinds (`a space is a music-room`); it carries the being's can-see / can-do / can-summon; (9) `I` is the Name (the actor/signer), never the being, a Name references itself as `I` (the self IS the Name), the being is referenced explicitly (`this being`, `my vessel`, by role/name), so there is no lowercase `i`-for-being to confuse and capital `I` aligns with English and rule 5; (10) articles mark kinds: `a` / `the` never precedes a specific being (it marks a role / space / matter or a generic instance), a specific being is bare (`Cherub`), reinforcing rule 5 on a second axis, and it holds at the predicate too (`Are you Bob?` = a being, `Are you a builder?` = a role); (11) a True Name (the key value) is never spoken in The Word, only three references point at Names: `I` (the actor), `Name` (the common noun, e.g. `mint a new Name`), and `I_AM` (the reality's root, owner-renameable); the root is the one Name with a speakable reference (shared origin), every other Name is reachable only as `I` or through its being; the key always lives in the act records (the God's-name resonance: known by its acts, only the origin named aloud); (12) the pulse is a being: every rhythm (a recurrence, a schedule, an hour passing) is some being's repeated act, watched by a `when`, no kernel timer, the harmony drummer was the first sketch; (13) native time is order (the chain's before/after) plus named bounded units (a closure act, `this was the first day`, becomes a handle), both clockless, while duration and human-labels come only from an optional clock being (the day-four apparatus) whose single escape hatch (`host: readClock`) is the one place the host clock touches the world, removing it leaves order / units / folds / federation intact; (14) a prohibition subtracts (`no one can back it`) and, where it meets a grant on the same act, the prohibition wins (a standing watch, rule 6; precedence flagged for Tabor); (15) a cross-world act names two places joined by `::` (`here :: there`), the left full and yours, the right possibly a bare position, the deed still the home Name's (rule 7), the `::` token flagged; (16) an ending seals a subject forward (`is done` / `is banished`: be:death, matter tombstone, name-banish), no new fact may ride it though every past fact stands (rule 7), distinct from rule 13 closing a span; a `be:release` is reversible, not an ending; (17) an act may name a receiver, the being it hands something to (`I give the drum to Claude`), a property on the DO act not a fourth noun, possession/place following the receiver via rule 2; (18) position is a being's own relation (`a being is in a space`, one at a time), a third relation beside contains/owns (rule 2); a being moves itself with a `walk` (a DO act, never BE), crossing to a foreign space via the rule-15 bridge, and is the sole mover of itself.

## Considering (not locked)

- **Clauses are the AST grain; verses retracted** (Tabor, 2026-06-16). Verses were editorial presentation; the operational atom is the **clause** (one subject, one verb, one operation), which is exactly the act-AST node already locked in the fact-chain resolution. So this sharpens Phase 1: the IR node is clause-grained, the parser targets clauses (the waw-consecutive `and X, and Y` is a sequence of nodes; nested clauses nest, e.g. a `say` carrying an inner act). Two corrections to the proposal, both from locked decisions: (1) a fact is NOT a separate authored clause-type (that is the node the consistency audit removed and the one-schema rule forbids); you author act-clauses plus declarations and closures, while facts and states are their recorded/folded reflections, rendered, not authored (`let there be light` is the act, `and there was light` is its effect). (2) A moment is ONE act + its facts (one stamper tick), not a grouping of several clauses; every grouping above the clause is a rule-13 named span (a day, a turn, a session). Tell: a clause's verb form picks its register, modal/general-present = law, past = life, present-state = the now (fold), `this was X` = closure (rule 13). Hierarchy: clause/act (one moment) -> named span (rule 13) -> reality. Changes none of the 18 rules. Grounded in the Hebrew root (Tabor's check, take as inspiration not authority): Biblical Hebrew narrative is a waw-consecutive chain of verb-initial clauses (the act-chain = life), and marks standing state with subject-first disjunctive clauses (`now the earth was formless` = the now/fold), so the act-vs-state register split is in the source grammar, not imposed; verse and chapter numbers are medieval editorial (rightly dropped), while the clause-chain and the day-refrain closures (rule 13) are in the text. Word order is presentation (Hebrew verb-first, our Word subject-first, same node).
